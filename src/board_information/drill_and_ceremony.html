What is close inteval?

Lateral space between Soldiers, measured from right to left by the Soldier on the right. The heel of the left hand is placed on the hip, even with the belt line, fingers and thumbs extended and facing downward, with the elbow in line with the body and touching the arm of the Soldier to their left

What is double interval?

Lateral space between Soldiers, measured from right to left by raising both arms shoulder high with fingers extended and joined (palms facing down) so that the fingertips are touching the fingerips of the Soldier to their left and right

What is normal interal?

Lateral space between Soldiers, measured from right to left by the Soldier on the right extending their left arm shoulder high, fingers and thumbs extended and joined, with the middle finger touching the right shoulder of the Soldier to the left

What is the history of "Retreat"

The term "retreat" is taken from the French word "retraite" and refers to the evening ceremony. The bugle call sounded at retreat was first used in the French army and it dates back to the Crusades. Retreat was sounded at sunset to notify sentries to start challenging until sunrise and to tell the ranks to go to their quarters. The old cavalry call "To the Standard" was in use since 1835 and has been replaced by the current call "To the Color". This remains as music honoring the flag as it is lowered in the evening

What is the history of "Reveille"?

Reveille was not originally intended as honors to the flag. In 1812, it was a drum call to signify that Soldiers should rise for day duty and sentries should leave off night challenging. As time passed, reveille came to denote when the flag was raised in the morning and honors were paid to it
