What are the rules for corrective training?

It must be appropriately tailored to curing the deficiency. It must be oriented to improving the Soldier's performance in their problem area. Can be conducted after normal duty hours, cannot be punishment, should continue only until deficiency is corrected, does not violate the Army's policies prohibiting hazing, bullying, and unlawful punishment

What Army Regulations cover corrective training?

AR 600-20 and AR 27-10

What are nonpunitive corrective measures?

They are the primary tools for teaching proper standards of conduct and performance

When do nonpunitive corrective measures constitute punishment?

These measures never constitute punishment. Punishment is given under UCMJ actions

When are nonpunitive corrective measures required before starting the nonjudicial punishment?

They are never required. The leader can skip corrective training and recommend an Article 15

Name four types of corrective training

Included among nonpunitive measures are denial of pass or other privilage, counseling, administrative reduction in grade, administrative reprimands and admonitions, extra training, bar to continued service, and military occupational specialty (MOS) reclassification

What is considered one of the most effective corrective measures?

Corrective training

Can exercise be used a corrective training?

Brief physical exercises are an acceptable form of corrective training for minor acts of indiscipline (for example, requiring the Soldier to do push-ups for arriving late to formation). The physical exercise cannot violate the Army's policies prohibiting hazing, bullying, and unlawful punishment

When is correction of a deficiency by corrective training required to be entered into the Soldier's official military record?

Never. Deficiencies satisfactorily corrected by means of training and instruction will not be noted in the official records of the Soldier
