When checking for breathing, why do you look at the chest?

You are looking for the equal rise and fall of the chest

What are the two methods for opening the airway?

Head tilt/chin lift and the jaw thrust method

What is the abdominal thrust used for?

Clearing a conscious casualty's airway obstruction

You are opening an airway on a casualty who may have a spinal injury. What technique should you not use?

Do NOT use the head tilt/chin lift method

Name the three parts of the respiratory system

1. Airway
2. Rib cage
3. Lungs

How do you determine the level of consciousness of a casualty

By using the AVPU method which means:
Alert responses to Voice
responds to Pain
Unresponsive

What are the steps when checking for breathing?

1. Look for the rise and fall of the chest
2. Listen for air escaping when exhaling
3. Feel for flow of air on the side of your face
4. Count the number of respirations for 15 seconds

What is the first thing you should do before administering first aid to a choking victim?

Ask them if they are choking

What are the two methods for clearing a choking hazard?

Abdominal thrust and chest thrusts

Name the two methods for rescue breathing?

Mouth to mouth and mouth to nose

During rescue breathing, how many breaths should an adult recieve in a minute?

12 to 20

During rescue breathing, how many breaths should a child recieve in a minute?

15 to 30
