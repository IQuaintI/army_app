What is your first general order?

I will guard everything within the limits of my post and quit my post only when properly relieved

What is your second general order?

I will obey my special orders and perform all my duties in a military manner

What is your third general order?

I will report violations of my special orders, emergencies, and anything not covered in my instructions to the commander of the relief

What are your special orders based on?

The current situation as well as the sensitivity of the site being protected

Define an interior guard

Detailed by commanders of military installations to protect property and enforce specific military regulations

Define exterior guard

Used to classify those guards outside of military installations, guards in combat areas, or those in hostile areas

If a guard observes any suspicious individual or individuals involved in a disturbance occuring on or near their post, what action should they take?

3. Offiers and NCOs of the day

Does a guard on a post require a challenge salute?

No

If a member of the military service finds an irresponsible guard on post, what must they do?

Notify the commander of the guard or the NCO of the guard and stay on the post until a qualified guard is posted

When does a guard surrender their weapon?

Only when ordered to do so by the guard chain of command

Anytime a guard is in doubt as to what action to take, what should they do?

Define escalation of force

Escalation of force is the series of sequential actions that begin with non-lethal forces (visual signals such as flags, spotlights, lasers, pyrotechnics) and may graduate to lethal means (Such as warnings, disabling, or deadly shots) to defeat the threat and protect the force

Does escalation of force override the rules of engagement?

No, the EOF measures complements and are implemented within the rules of engagement

What is the punishment for any person that discloses a countersign or patrol word?

Any person, who in times of war, who discloses the patrol word or countersign to any person not entitled to recieve it, shall be punished by death or such other punishment as a court-martial may direct

How should the countersign or patrol words be given by a guard after the person is advanced to be recognized

The challenge and password are given in a hushed tone to prevent them from being overheard by others

How should a guard use the challenge?

In a sentence to prevent the enemy from knowing exactly what the challenge word is

When announcing the challenge, how should the guard be positioned if possible?

If possible, the guard should be out of sight when challenging

If a group approaches a guard post, how many are allowed to approach the position at one time?

Only one member of the group will approach for identification

Define Field Officer of the Day

A field grade officer detailed as the personal representative of the commanding officer

Define Officer of the Day

an officer acting directly under the commanding officer or field officer of the day who is responsible for the execution of all orders of the commanding officer realting to guard duty or any other assignmed duties

Define Staff Duty Officer of the Day

SDO is an officer acting as the appointed representative of the commander when the commander is not present or after the end of the duty day

Define Commander of the Guard

The senior officer or NCO of the guard. Responsible for the instruction, discipline, and performance of the duty of the guard

Define Sergeant of the Guard

The senior NCO of the guard. Supervises the enlisted members of the guard and is responsible to the commander of the guard

Define the Commander of the Relief

Next senior NCO of the guard. Responsible for the performance of all guards in their relief for the duration of the relief's tour of duty. Tasked with inspecting each guards relief and ensuring that the relief is performing their duties. COR is responsible to the Sergeant of the Guard

Define relief

An individual guard or group of guards that are assigned to perform guard duty

Define a guard

An individual responsible to keep watch over, protect, shield, defend, warn, or other duties presrcibed by general orders and special orders

What is the commander's primary means of enforcing federal, state, and local laws?

When is a countersign used?

The countersign is used in combat enviroments and when directed by the commander in garrison

When selecting countersigns and patrol runs, how should they be selected?

Select words that are difficult for the enemy to pronounce and do not indicate an expected answer. Selected sounds should be distinct but similar to those heard locally

What is a patrol word?

A secret word shared only with those persons entitled to inspect the guard and to commanders and members of the guard. It is used as a check on the counter-sign. The parole word is used by authorized persons to inspect the guard

What is considered the best means of identification

The authorized access badge is considered the best means of identification where doubt exists

Explain how a patrol word is used?

When used during the hours of challenging, the inspector does not give the patrol word until he/she is advanced to a position near the guard. Afterwards, the inspector uses the patrol word and the guard returns the countersign. If the inspector uses the countersign, the guard returns the patrol word. The inspector then identifies themselves
