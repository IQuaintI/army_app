A Soldier successfully graduates Airborne School and receives "blood wings" from the squad. The Soldier is fine with the activity. Does this behavior still constitute as hazing?

This is still hazing even if the Soldier agreed to the ritual as it is still in violation of Army policy

Typically hazing is normally directed at individuals who have recently ___?

Achieved a career milestone

What actions may be taken against a Soldier who HAZED another Soldier?

The Soldier can be punished under UCMJ

Define hazing?

A form of harassment that includes conduct through which Soldiers or DA Civilians employees (who haze Soldiers), without a proper military authority or other governmmental purpose but with a nexus to military service that physically or psychologically injure Soldiers for the purpose of initiation, admission, affiliation, change in status, or continuence of membership in an organization

If one Soldier coerces another Soldier into participating in an act of hazing, how is this viewed in regards to AR 600-20?

This is still considered hazing

When is an act of hazing most likely to occur?

During a rite of passage or congratulatory act

Must hazing involve physical contact to be considered Hazing?

Hazing does not need physical contact, it can also be be verbal or psychological

The following statement defines what act: bruises, swelling, broken bones, internal injuries, piercing of a Soldier's skin in any manner, forcing or requiring consumption of drugs, food, water, or encouraging illegal, harmful, demeaning, or dangerous acts

Hazing

What actions, when authorized by the chain of command, is NOT considered hazing?

1. Physical and mental hardship associated with operations and training
2. Lawful punishment under UCMJ
3. Administrative corrective measures
4. Valid corrective training
5. Physical training
6. Other similar activities authorized by the chain of command

Where can a Soldier who is being exposed to hazing or bullying make an official complaint?

1. The chain of command
2. Commander
3. Law enforcement
4. The IG

What action must a Soldier take if they have knowledge of a bullying or hazing incident?

They must report it to their chain of command

What activities can be considered hazing even if the Soldier is not present?

1. Phone message
2. Text message
3. Email
4. Social media or any other virtual or electronic medium

What is the difference between bullying and hazing?

Hazing is event-specific, such as "Blood Wings", whereas bullying will continue until it is stopped by outside intervention

Can a subordinate haze or bully a superior?

Yes

Name four acts that are typically associated with hazing

1. Any form of initiation or congratulatory act that involves physically striking, beating, paddling, whipping, or burning another person in any manner or threatening to do the same
2. Pressing objects into another persons skin, regardless if it pierces the skin, such as "pinning" or "tacking on" of rank insignia, aviator wings, jump wings, diver insignia, badges, medals, or any other insignia
3. Oral or written berating of another person with the purpose of belittling or humiliating
4. Encouraging another person to engage in illegal, harmful, demeaning, or dangerous acts
5. Playing abusive or malicious tricks
6. Excessive physical exercise
7. Confinement to restricted areas, isolation, or sleep-deprivation
8. Brading, handcuffing, duct taping, tattoing, shaving, greasing, or pantsing another individual
9. Subjecting another person to excessive or abusive use of water or forcing them to consume drugs, food, alcohol, or other substances

If a Soldier agrees to be object of thr act of hazing or bullying, is the act therefore acceptable?

No. Willingness to participate as a victim is irrelevant - it is still in violation of AR 600-20

Where are bullying and hazing incidents recorded for tracking purposes?

The Equality Opportunity Office

What action will a commander take when a Soldier is the perpetrator of a hazing or bullying incident?

The commander will ensure that the security manager properly records the derogatory information as an incident report

What standard will be used to determine if an activity is hazing?

The Reasonable Person Rule

How can an electronic communication be used to inflict harm?

By sharing or posting signs, writing, images, sounds, or data
