What action must be taken when a Soldier receives a punitive action for a sex related offense?

The commander will ensure the Soldier's performance discipline folder (Army Military Human Resource Record) is annotated when a court martial, non-judicial punishment, or punitive administrative action for a sex related offense is imposed. This information cannot be filed in the restricted folder b the commander.

When will it be recorded in a Soldier's official record that they refused to take a polygraph tests?

Never

What is the maximum amount of time a memoranda designated for the local file ca remain on the local file?

No more than 18 months or until re-assignment to another general court martial convening authority.

Can a letter of reprimand be processes on a Soldier that has left the unit?

Yes. A supervisor can have a written letter with intent to reprimand and the losing and gaining commanders can coordinate on the punishment.

Who is responsible for ensuring te reprimand is removed from the local file after the maximum 18 month filing period?

It is both the Soldier and the units responsibility

Can a commander impose a punishment after leaving?

Yes, the commander may leave information regarding the Solder's situation to the gaining commander who may then consider the punishment.

How many days does a Soldier have to make a statement regarding a reprimand?

Seven calender days

What action will a commander take if they do not believe that someone is capable of maintaining a security clearance?

The commander will follow the guidelines presented in AR 380-67 for reporting the information and will notify the supporting counter-intelligence outfit.

What does POSTA stand for?

Positions of significant trust and authority

What does PASS stand for?

Positions or appointments screened for suitability

How is an Army suitability evaluation conducted?

The board will typically consist of colonel ranked officers (however anyone of higher rank than the recipient is also acceptable)and at least one sergeant major should the individual under evaluation be enlisted. Members of the board will not vote on cases they were personally a part of or in which they have personal bias.

Can a Soldier appeal a DASEB?

Yes, if the Soldier believes that information filed in their AMHRR is untrue or unjust they may present sufficient evidence to the board.

Can a Soldier request that an Article 15 be moved to their restricted personnel file?

Yes, if the Article 15 has completed it's intended purpose a Soldier may request it be moved to the restricted file. The appeal must also include that the Soldier has received at least one evaluation since its imposition

In cases where the Soldiers chain of command which imposed the Article 15 no longer exist, who will support the decision to transfer the Article 15 to the restricted file?

The Soldiers next hirer commanding authority may support the transfer.

If a soldier's request to move unfavorable information to their restricted file is initially denied, what action can they take?

Soldiers who have an appeal denied may submit a request for reconsideration no earlier than one year from the date of notification of denial. The request must include evidence not previously considered.

If a soldier who directed the unfavorable information be filed in the soldier's file later discovers that the information that was the basis for the action was untrue, what action may they take?

An officer who directed the filing in the file of an administrative memorandum of reprimand may request its revision or removal if later investigation determines such information is untrue in part or in whole.

What is the time limit for submitting an appeal request removal of unfavorable information from the file?

There is no time restriction for submitting an appeal for removal of unfavorable information from the file.
