What are the events for the ACFT?

1. Three Repetitions Maximum Deadlift
2. Standing Power Throw
3. Hand release Push-ups
4. Sprint-Drag-Carry
5. Leg Tuck
6. Two Mile Run

Give two examples of Soldiers tasks that the three Repetition Maximum Deadlift measures?

1. Lifting heavy loads from the ground
2. Lift and carry a casualty on a litter
3. Lift and move personnel and equipment

Give two examples of the Soldier tasks that the Standing Power Throw measures?

1. Jumping across a ditch
2. Executing a buddy drag
3. Throwing equipment over an obstacle
4. Assisting a buddy to climb up a wall
5. Loading equipment
6. Progressive levels of force in hand-to-hand combat

Give two examples of the Soldier tasks that the Hand Release Push-up measure?

1. Hand-to-hand combat
2. Pushing loads and vehicle
3. Getting to and from the ground

Give two examples of the Soldier tasks that the Sprint-Drag-Carry measure?

1. React to direct and indirect fire
2. Build a hasty fighting position
3. Extracting a casualty

Give two examples of the Soldier tasks that the Leg Tuck measure?

1. Surmounting obstacles and walls
2. Ruck march
3. Infiltration

What does the acronym H2F stand for?

Holistic Health and Fitness System

Name four areas that the ACFT assesses with regards to physical readiness?

1. Muscular strength
2. Muscular endurance
3. Aerobic endurance
4. Explosive power and anaerobic capacity
5. Speed
6. Agility
7. Flexibility
8. Balance
9. Coordination

The ACFT trains and tests the physical capabilities of the Soldier and it also assesses what other capabilities?

Mental toughness

What does the ACFT assess with regard to fatigue?

It tests if a Soldier can move under fatigue, their ability to concentrate, and to persist

Explain the self-administered ACFT

There are no self-administered ACFTs

How many Soldiers are required to administer the ACFT to one Soldier?

3

Name the three individual positions required to administer the ACFT to a Soldier

OIC or NCOIC and 2 graders

If there is only one grader available to administer the ACFT, who may serve as the second grader?

The OIC or NCOIC

During the ACFT, how does a grader count?

They count the correct number of repetitions out loud

For timed ACFT events, how many stopwatches are required?

Two, a primary and a backup

How many minutes does the Soldier have for the Leg Tuck?

Two minutes

A 16 lane ACFT test site provides the capacity to test how many Soldiers in 120 minutes or less?

64

What is the restriction when conducting the Sprint-Drag-Carry indoors?

It will not be performed on unimproved dirt or gravel surfaces, rubberized track, gym floors, ice, or snow

An ACFT testing area of 30x50 meters allows for how many test lanes and what are the dimensions of each lane?

30 meters by 50 meters

How long does the test area for the ACFT have to be?

30 meters by 50 meters

What are the requirements for the test site?

An area measuring 30m x 50m on grass or artificial turf, free of any significant hazards - an area that can be used to conduct drill

What are the course requirements for the 2 mile run?

A largely flat running course that has a solid surface with no more than a 3 percent uphill grade and has no overal decline. The start and finish must be the same altitude

What are the minimum and maximum weight requirements for the Repetition Deadlift?

140 lbs. and 340 lbs.

How many stop watches should be available for the 2 Mile Run>

A minimum of two and a maximum of 17

What are the time restrictions for conducting the ACFT?

The events must be completed in order on the same day during a test period not to exceed 120 minutes to include the preparation and recovery drills

If a Soldier fails the Maximum Deadlift, do they still have to take the remainder of the exam?

Yes, the Soldier must attempt all six events

How much rest is given after the Leg Tuck and when does it start?

10 minutes and it starts when the last Soldier has completed the Leg Tuck

WHat is the maximum amount of Soldiers that can rotate through a lane at once?

4

How much rest is given between ACFT events?

There are no programmed rests outside of the 10-minute rest prior to the run. The exception being a 5-minute rest between events when there are either one or two Soldiers being tested

How many restarts are allowed during the ACFT?

There are no restarts

When must questions concerning grading or other issues regarding the ACFT be resolved?

All questions must be resolved by the OIC or NCOIC within the 120-minute time limit

How is a video recording used during the ACFT?

It can be used for training purposes but it will not be used to adjudicate

A Soldier states a grader incorrectly scored an event. The unit is recording the ACFT. The recording shows that the scorer made a mistake. What action must the unit take to correct the score?

The unit will take no action. The score remains the same as the recording cannot be used to adjudicate the event scores

What items are not authorized for wear during the ACFT?

1. Any piece of clothing not part of the APFU
2. Devices or equipment that offer any potential for unfair advantage
3. Nasal strips
4. Back braces
5. Elastic bandages
6. Limb braces

What electronic devices are eligible for wear during the ACFT?

1. Biometric measuring devices such as watches
2. Heart rate monitors
3. Step counter and fitness tracker
4. No other electronic devices are authorized

When can a Soldier use gloves during the ACFT?

As long as the gloves meet the requirements of AR 670-1, Soldiers may wear them for any event and at any time of the year in any climate

Which Soldiers will go first during the Standing Power Throw?

The Soldier who achieved the lowest weight on the Maximum Deadlift. The order remains the same for the remaining events

What do graders issue each Soldier for the 2-Mile Run?

Graders will issue each Soldier a vest or a number

What does the Soldier's signature on the ACFT scorecard validate?

The Soldier's signature means they concur with the score

What is the maximum score for each ACFT event?

60 points in each event for a minimum passing score of 360 points

What is the maximum score a Soldier can earn on the ACFT?

600 points

How many throws will the Soldier perform during the Standing Power Throws?

2

During the Maxium Deadlift if during the first attempt the Soldier successfully completes the attempt, what action may that they take?

They can attempt to lift a higher weight

During the three Maximum Deadlift if a Soldier fails on the first attempt, what action may they take?

They are allowed to use a lower weight

What happens if a Solder recieves a safety stop during the first attempt of the MDL?

The grader will inform the Soldier of the error and tell the Soldier to stop. Then the Soldier will be allowed to resume the attempt from the beginning

Does a safety stop during the first repetition of the MDL count?

No

If a Soldier recieves a second safety stop, what action does the grader take?

The grader will stop the Soldier, explain the error, and then record a failed attempt. The Soldier is allowed to take a lower weight

Give three examples when the MDL attempt be terminated?

1. Drops the bar on the ground
2. Removes their hands from the bar between repititions when the bar is on the ground
3. Fails to touch the bar to the ground between reptitions
4. Does not perform a continous movement by resting on the ground
5. Is called for a safety stop on the 2nd or 3rd repedition of an attempt

Standing Power Throw results are recorded to the nearest ___

Decimeter

How is the ball landing measurement taken for the Standing Power Throw?

Taken from the center of the ball's landing point perpendicular to the tape measure to the nearest decimeter

If a Soldier faults on two attempts of the SPT, what action is taken?

The Soldier is given one final attempt to score a record throw and it will be recorded as the second 2nd throw

Give three examples of when a repitition will not count during the Hand Release Push-ups?

1. Failing to maintain a straight body
2. Failing to fully extend elbows in the up position
3. Failing to bring the hands back to the starting position to complete the repetition
4. Failing to keep the feet within a boot's width apart

Give three examples of when the Hand Release Push-ups event will be terminated?

1. Deviates from the straight body alignment while in the front leaning rest
2. Lifts a foot or hand from the ground
3. Places a knee on the ground from the front lean and position
4. Repeats the hand release movement before raising up from the ground
5. Rests on the ground
