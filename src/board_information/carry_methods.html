When should manual carries be used to move a casualty with a neck or spine injury?

Only in life threatening situations, otherwise the manual carry should not be used

Name the three types of manual first aid carry positions

1. Fireman's carry
2. Neck drag
3. Cradle-drop drag
4. Litters

What are the two types of improvised litters?

Poncho and jacket

If a spinal injury is suspected, how should a sling be applied?

It should not be tied around the casualty's neck - it should be pinned to the casualty's clothing

What piece of equipment may be used to assist in pulling a casualty from a vehicle if an upward movement is required?

A pistol belt or similar material

When is a fireman's carry used in first aid encounters?

For unconscious or severely injured casualties

When is a neck drag carry used in first aid encounters?

In combat for short distances

When is a craddle-drop drag carry used in first aid encounters?

To move a casualty who cannot walk when being moved up or down stairs

When should the litter carry be used in the first aid encounters?

If materials are available, or if the casualty must be moved a long distance, or if manual carries will incur greater injury
