Substance Abuse Program (AR 600-85)

What are the two overarching tenets of the ASAP program?

Prevention and treatment

What action will be taken for a SOldier who fails to participate in or respond successfully to rehabilitation in the ASAP program?

They will be processed for separation

What does ASAP stand for?

Army Substance Abuse Program

If a Soldier has an alcohol or drug related incident of misconduct at any time during the 12-month period following successful completion of teh ASAP program, what action will be taken?

The Soldier will be separated as an alcohol or drug abuse rehabilitation failure

What are the requirements placed upon a Soldier if they choose to consume alcohol?

They must consume alcohol in a lawful and responsible manner

When is a Soldier considered impaired by alcohol when on duty?

When the blood alcohol content is equal to or greater than .50 grams of alcohol per 100 milliliters of blood

What action will be taken for a Soldier who is found to be drinking underage?

They will be referred to ASAP fro screening within 5 days

Define an ASAP rehabilitation failure

Includes Soldiers with a subsequent alcohol or drug related incident of misconduct at any time during the 12 months period following successful completion of ASAP or during the 12-month period following removal from the program

If a Soldier is called for an unannounced alert at 0430 but was not originally due to report until 0630, can they be administered an alcohol screening test?

No, if the Soldier was not informed of the early report time they cannot be tested until the time they would normally have reported to work

What action will be taken for a Soldier that tests positive for illicit drugs for the first time?

They will be processed for separation from service within 30 calender days of the commander receiving notification

If a Soldier is retained after first time drug use and then tests positive again, what action will be taken?

Chain of Command will initiate administrative separation of the Soldier

How much time will a Soldier have to report for testing once notified?

Two hours

If a Soldier reports to the testing area, when or how are they allowed to depart he testing area?

Only when authorized by the commander to do so and they should be provided an NCO ro Officer escort while away from the testing area

WHat does the acronym UPL stand for?

Unit Prevention Leader

What are the requirements for an individual to be selected as an observer?

1. Be an officer, warrant officer, or NCO (E-5 or above)
2. Same gender as the Soldier being observed
3. Unimpeachable moral character
4. Maturity to preserve the dignity of the Soldier being tested
5. Not enrolled in ASAP
6. Not under investigation for legal, administrative, or substance abuse related offenses

What are the duties of the observer?

1. Maintain direct eye contact with specimen bottle from the time the UPL hands it to the Soldier until the time the UPL places it in the box
2. Observe the urine leaving the Soldier's body and enter the specimen bottle
3. Ensure no on tampers with teh Soldier's specimen
4. Guide the Soldier through the collection process
5. Report unusual occurrences and attempts to adulterate the specimen to the UPL

What actions are directed for a Soldier that cannot provide a sample?

They should drink at least 8 ounces of fluid every half-hour, not to exceed 40 ounces. Soldiers will remain in the holding area until they are ready to provide a specimen

When a Soldier is informed, they will provide a sample what does this constitute?

A lawful order

What are the methods of referral for the ASAP program?

1. Voluntary (Self ID)
2. Commander directed
3. Drug testing ID
4. Alcohol incident
5. Medical ID
6. Investigation and apprehension

What is the most desirable method of referral to the ASAP program?

Self-referral

What are the rehabilitation levels?

Level 1 - Nonresidential/outpatient rehab
Level 2 - Partial in-patient/residential treatment

Define the limited use policy?

Facilitates self-identification and allows a Soldier to get help and make a new start without being punished for past offenses

Can a Soldier be deployed while in a rehabilitation program?

Yes, if the commander deems it necessary

What action will be taken for any Soldier who is involved in two serious alcohol related misconducts in a 12-month period?

The Soldier will be processed for separation

What action will be taken for any Soldier convicted of DWI or DUI two times in their career?

The Soldier will be processed for separation

If a Soldier has tested positive and is pending separation, is the Soldier exempt from further testing?

No, the Soldier is not exempt from continued testing because they are pending separation or have tested positive on a previous drug test

What action will be taken for Soldiers diagnosed as drug dependant?

They will be offered rehabilitation prior to separation

Provide examples of smart testing

1. Random testing
2. Back-to-back testing
3. Weekend/holiday testing
4. During field exercises
5. End of duty day

How many UPLs will deploy with a unit?

At least two

When are non-testing personnel permitted in the holding area?

Never they are barred from the testing area

What is the window a commander can provide for reporting o the testing station?

Within two hours of notification but no longer than six hours

What action will be taken with regards to the latrines prior to testing?

1. THey will be inspected by the UPL
2. Remove any possible adulterants
3. Ensure Soldiers have soap and paper towels to wash their hands
4. Place OFF LIMITS signs to on-testing personnel sign on the door

If a SOldier does not provide a specimen within a reasonable period of time, but not less than three hours of reporting to the site, what action may the commander take?

Refer the Soldier to medical evaluation

If a Soldier's specimen comes up positive, what recourse does the Soldier have?

The Soldier may request a retest at a commercial laboratory, at the Solder's own expense, when a sufficient quality of the same specimen is available for retesting

If a UPL or observer suspects that a Soldier adulterated a specimen, what action is taken?

1. UPL secures the specimen bottle and its content
2. UPL completes the collection process but does nto release the Soldier
3. UPL will have another observer or NCO to notify the commander and the UPL will explain the situation to the commander

When a Soldier is identified as a probable alcohol or other drug abuser, what action must the commander take?

Coordinate with law enforcement to see if the commander should conduct an initial interview of the alcohol or drug abuser

What are the rehabilitation objectives of the ASAP program?

1. Return the Soldiers to full duty as soon as possible
2. Identify Soldiers that cannot be rehabilitated
3. Assist and refer Soldiers who cannot be rehabilitated in ASAP to a rehab facility in the vicinity where they reside after separation
4. Help resolve alcohol and other drug abuse problems within the family

If a Soldier is command referred to ASAP and enrolled in ASAP and fails to attend mandatory training, it may constitute violation of what?

Article 86 of the UCMJ

Define "smart testing"

Random testing conducted in such a manner that is unpredictable by the testing population

When deploying a unit, how much in supplies will the unit take in regards to testing?

Enough supplies to test 100% of their assigned strength throughout the entire deployment cycle

Describe the testing area

1. Setup preferably in a non-carpeted area
2. UPLs back to the wall
3. Latrines as close as possible
4. Testing are is controller where only testing and command personnel are present

If a Soldier does not provide a specimen and is cleared by medical personnel, what action should the commander take?

The commander should consult with the Judge Advocate
