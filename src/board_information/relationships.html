Relationships between Soldiers of different ranks can be percieved to exhibit __

Familiarity

Provide two examples of behavior that could be considered as an inappropriate relationship between Soldiers of different ranks (actual or percieved)

1. Repeated bar visits
2. Nightclubs
3. Eating establishment
4. Visiting homes

What types of gatherings between Soldiers of different ranks are permitted and would not violate the policies of AR 600-20 concerning relationships?

Social gatherings that involve the entire unit, office, or section

Name two of the five standards used to determine if a relationship between Soldiers of different ranks is prohibited under the provisions of AR 600-20

1. Compromise or percieved compromise of supervision and authority
2. Actual or percieved unfairness
3. Actual or percieved improper use of grade for personal gain
4. Actual or percieved exploitation or coercion
5. Creates an actual or percieved adverse impact on discipline, authority, morale, or the ability of the command to accomplish the mission

Who is held accountable for participating in an inappropriate relationship between Soldiers of different ranks?

All members of the relationship are held accountable

What is the expectation to the "sharing of living accomodations rule" between Soldiers of different ranks?

Shared living accomodations between Soldiers of different ranks are authorized for operational requirements

Who is in the best position to terminate an inappropriate relationship between Soldiers of different ranks?

The senior member of the relationship

Name two types of relationships between between Soldiers of different ranks that are prohibited by AR 600-20

1. Buisness relationships
2. Dating, sharing living space, fraternization prior to marriage
3. An intimate or sexual relationship
4. A relationship that adheres to 600-20 and then the status of an individual changes (Two enlisted are dating and one commissions therefore the relationship must end or marriage must occur)

There is an appropriate relationship between an officer and an NCO, the couple then marries. Can action be taken by the commander?

The marriage does not prevent command action for fraternization
