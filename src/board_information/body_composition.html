Army Body Composition Program (AR 600-9)

What regulation covers the Army Body Composition Program?

AR 600-9

The minimum weight standard for a male Soldier that is 17 years old and 58 inches tall is 91 pounds. Your male Soldier is 17 years old and 58 inches tall. He weighs 89 pounds. Should any action be taken?

Yes, the commander must immedietely refer the soldier for a medical evaluation

What does the acronym ABCP mean?

Army Body Composition Program

What is the primary objective of the Army Body Composition Program?

To ensure all Soldiers achieve and maintain optimal wellbeing and performance under all conditions

How often will Soldiers be screened to ensure compliance with AR 600-9?

At a minimum, Soldiers will be screened every six months

What action will a commander take if a Soldier exceeds the body fat standard in accordance with AR 600-9?

The commander will flag the Soldier in accordance with AR 600-8-2

What tool do commanders use to screen Soldiers for their semi-annual body fat assessment?

The authorized screening table for their age and measured height

When is a Soldier released from the ABCP?

When they meet the body fat standard of AR 600-9

What action must be taken when a Soldier exceeds the body fat standard?

They must recieve a notification counseling and be flagged in accordance with AR 600-8-2. This must be accomplished within three working days

What are some of the ramifications if a Soldier is flagged?

1. The soldier is in a non-promotable status
2. Cannot be assigned to command or command sergeant major, or first sergeant positions
3. Are not authorized to attend military schooling

When enrolled in the ABCP, how long does the Soldier have to read TG 358?

14 Days

When is the screening weight used to release a Soldier from ABCP?

Never; the body fat standard is always used to release a Soldier from the ABCP

How long does the Soldier have to acknowledge the memorandum of enrollment in the ABCP?

Two working days from the date of notification

How long does the Soldier have to acknowledge the memorandum of enrollment in the ABCP?

It must be accomplished within 14 days of the notification counseling

With regards to AR 600-9, what does the acronym SAP stand for?

Soldier Action Plan

Can a Soldier modify their weight loss plan while enrolled in the ABCP?

Yes

How often will ABCP assessments be conducted?

Approximately every 30 days

What is the purpoes of the monthly ABCP assessment?

To measure a Soldier's progress in the ABCP program and document the results on the DA Form 5500 or 5501

Define satisfactory progress in the ABCP?

The monthly loss of three to eight pounds or one percent body fat

What actions are taken if a Soldier haas a temporary medical condition with regards to the ABCP enrollment?

The Soldier will be flagged; recieve nutrition counseling, and participate in monthly body fat assessments

If a Soldier's temporary medical condition is not corrected within 6-12 months, what action must the Soldier take?

The Soldier must continue to participate in the ABCP, but must show satisfactory progress

How is failure in the ABCP defined?

If a Soldier exhibits less than satisfactory progress on two consecutive monthly assessments, or after six months in the ABCP and they still exceed body fat standards and have exhibited less than satisfactory progress for three or more nonconsecutive monthly assessments

How long is a pregnant Soldier exempt from the standards of AR 600-9?

They are exempt for the duration of the pregnancy plus 180 days after the pregnancy ends

What are the qualifications for indiviudals taking body circumference?

These individuals must be designated unit fitness trainers, certified master fitness trainers and/or trained in body circumference methodology as specified in AR 600-9

What action is taken if a female is not available to tape another female?

No action can be taken until another female is present to tape the female

How are circumference measurements taken?

All circumference measurements will be taken sequentially three times and recorded to the nearest half-inch

What undergarments are not authorized for wear during the taping process?

Any undergarment that may serve to bind the abdomen, hips, or thigh areas are not authorized. This includes but is not limited to spandex shorts or girdle like undergarments

Circumference measurements are taken to the nearest ___ inch?

Half inch

A Soldiers height is measured to the nearest ___ inch?

Half-inch

When taking height measurements, a Soldier's height is measured to the nearest half-inch, however it is then rounded to the nearest ___?

Inch

In accordance with AR 600-9, what action will be taken if a Soldier is in the ABCP program and PCS's to another unit

The commander must foward a complete ABCP file to the Soldier's gaining unit

What action can a commander take if a Soldier does not present a soldierly appearance?

The commander can direct a body fat assessment to determine if the Soldier exceeds the screening table for their weight or meassured height

How many days can be allowed between an APFT and a weigh-in?

Commanders are encouraged to allow a minimum of seven days between an APFT and weigh-in

Why does AR 600-9 recommend a minimum of seven days between an APFT and the weigh-in?

Soldiers exceeding their screening weight may attempt to lose weight quickly and this may result in them being unable to perform at their best during an APFT

Who will measure a Soldier?

A trained person of the same gender

If a TRAINED person of the same gender is not available to measure the Soldier, what action will be taken?

A female Soldier will be present when a male measures a female; a male Soldier will be present when a female measures a male

What services will be provided when a Soldier is enrolled in the ABCP?

They will be provided:
1. Exercise guidance by the Unit Master Fitness Trainer
2. Nutrition counseling by a registered dietitian or health care provider
3. Assistance in behavioral modification

How many working days does the commander have to COUNSEL the Soldier of enrollment in the ABCP?

Two working days from the initiation of the flag

What actions are required at the meeting with the dietitian?

The Soldier must bring a copy of the commander's request for nutrition counseling and the Soldier's Plan of Action to the dietitian, and provide the retrieval code from the interactive questionnare

What publication must the Soldier confirm to the commander that they have read in their Soldier Action Plan?

USAPHC TG358

What does the Soldier do with the retrieval code from the interactive questionnare?

The Soldier should provide this code to the dietitian or healthcare provider so the responses can be reviewed and the provider can provide feedback

How long after the enrollment in the ABCP does a Soldier have to meet a dietitian?

It must be completed within 30 days of enrollment in the ABCP

After completing the nutritional counseling what must a Soldier provide to the commander?

A memorandum signed by the dietitian or healthcare provider verifying the nutritional counseling took place

name two of the four conditions when a medical evaluation is required by the ABCP?

1. When requested by the unit commander
2. When requested by the Soldier
3.When the Soldier is being considered for seperation for failure to make satisfactory progress
4. When the Soldier is within six months of ETS after the intitiation of a reenlistment bar for failure to make sufficient progress

What action can be taken if a Soldier has a temporary medical condition that causes weight gain or prevents weight or body fat loss that will take longer than 6 months to resolve?

The initial 6-month period can be extended by a physician for up to 12 months

What action is taken against a Soldier who fails to make satisfactory progress in the ABCP when they have a temporary medical condition?

The Soldier will not be penalized for failing to show satisfactory progress. However, if the Soldier meets body fat standards during this timeframe, they will be removed from the ABCP

What action will a commander take if a Soldier in the ABCP is a program failure with no underlying medical condition?

The commander will initiate seperation action, bar to reenlistment, or involuntary transfer to the IRR

What action will the commander take if a Soldier in the ABCP is a program failure with an underlying medical condition?

The Soldier will be issued a temporary profile and will not be required to make satisfactory progress for 6 to 12 months as determined by a physician. The Soldier will participate in the ABCP while on temporary profile

What action will be taken against a soldier with no underlying medical condition, who exceeds the body fat standard within 12 months of release from the ABCP?

The Soldier will be processed for separation, barred from reenlistment, or transferred to the IRR

What action will be taken against a Soldier with no underlying medical condition, who exceeds the body fat standard after 12 months but less than 36 months from the date of release from the ABCP?

The commander will initiate a flag, re-enroll the Soldier in the ABCP and the Soldier will have 90 days to meet the standard

What action is taken when a Soldier in the ABCP desires to reenlist or extend?

No soldier in the ABCP will be allowed to reenlist or extend. Note in rare cases the General Courts Martial Convening Authority or 1st General Officer in the chain of command can make an exception to policy

How many people are required to take the circumference measurememnts? Explain their duties

Two trained individuals; one to place the tape measure and determine measurements and the other to assure proper placement and tensions of tape

What action is taken if during the taping process any one of the three measurements differs by more than one inch?

An additional measurement will be taken, compute a mathematical average of the three measurements with the least difference to the nearest half-inch and record this value

In what uniform will tape measurements be taken?

Stocking feet in the Army physical fitness uniform trunks and t-shirt

What type of tape measure must be used during the taping process?

It must be of a non-stretchable material, preferably fiberglass. Cloth or steel tapes are unacceptable

How often will scales be calibrated?

Annually

When weight measurements for the body fat assessment the weight will be rounded to the nearest?

Pound

What areas are measured during the body fat assessment on a male Soldier?

The neck, wasit (abdomen), and hips

How are consecutive measurements taken?

The Soldier measuring will take a complete set of all measurements and then begin the process again. This discourages the assumption of a repeated measurement

How is the abdomen measured in relationship to the navel?

The measurement is taken at the navel level and parallel to the floor with arms at the side

What should leaders look for in a Soldier that might be close or over their screening wieght or body fat percentage?

Leaders should look for unsafe weight loss strategies; the commander should refer these Soldieres to their primary care manager

If a Soldier reduces their calorie intake by 500 calories per day, what is the expected result?

It should result in the loss of approximately one pound per week

What does body composition consist of?

Body composition consist of two major elements: lean body mass and body fat

What parts of the body fall in to the lean body muscle category?

Muscle, bone, and essential organ tissue

Describe how the Soldier will stand when taking a height measurement?

The Soldier will stand on a flat surface, head held horizontal, looking directly foward with the line of vision horizontal, and chin parallel to the floor. The body will be straight but not rigid, similar to the position of attention

To achieve active weight loss, how many minutes of physical activity are required daily?

60-90 minutes

Name three of five unsafe weight loss strategies as listed in AR 600-9?

1. Fasting or starvation
2. Water loss or forced dehydration
3. Abuse of diuretics or laxatives
4. Vomiting or purging
5. Use of diet or weight loss pills

What is a Soldier's individual responsibility in accordance with AR 600-9?

Each Soldier is responsible for meeting the standards of AR 600-9

What action must the commander take if a Soldier is flagged as a result of the ABCP program?

The commander must conduct monthly review of all Soldiers who are flagged or have been flagged within the past 36 months for failing to meet body standards

What are the duties and responsibilites of the unit fitness training NCO or master fitness trainer?

1. Provide proper exercise and fitness techniques to assist Soldiers in meeting and maintaining ABCP programs
2. Assist commanders in developing physical fitness programs
3. Train other NCOs in proper height, weight, and body circumference methods to assess body fat composition

Name three of the five exceptions to the ABCP?

1. Soldiers with major limb loss
2. Soldiers on established continued on active duty and/or continued on active reserve status
3. Pregnant and postpartum Soldiers
4. Soldiers who have been hospitalized for 30 continuous days or greater
5. New recruits

What action can a Soldier request if they do not meet one of the five exemptions to AR 600-9?

They may request a temporary exception to policy AR 600-9 standards

If a new recruit exceeds body fat standards after 180 days, what action will be taken?

The Soldier will be flagged and enrolled in the ABCP

What is the effective date of enrollment in the ABCP?

The date of Soldier is notified by the unit commander that they have been entered into the program

What actions must be documented in the Soldier Action Plan?

1. The Soldier should read TG 358
2. Provide the date and time of scheduled nutritonal counseling
3. Indicate what action they plan to take towards meeting the body fat standards
4. Complete the Army Move 123 interactive questionnaire, review the results, and record the retrieval code

Once the Soldier has developed their Soldier Action Plan, when can it be modified?

The Soldier may modify the plan at any time while enrolled in the ABCP

Name four of nine documents that are contained in a complete ABCP packet?

1. DA Form 268
2. DA Form 5500 or DA Form 5501
3. Notification counseling
4. Soldier Action Plan
5. Nutritional counseling results memorandum
6. Medical evaluation request memorandum
7. Medical evaluation results if indicated
8. Release from ABCP counseling memorandum
9. Form 3349

What actions are taken when a Soldier has a temporary medical condition which causes weight gain or prevents fat loss?

The Soldier will have up to six months from date of medical evaluation to undergo treatment and resolve the issue (can be extended to 12 months). The Soldier will participate in the ABCP to be include being flagged, nutritional counseling, and monthly assessments, but is not required to show satisfactory progress during the medical evaluation period

If a Soldier is enrolled in the ABCP and becomes pregnant, when will the Soldier be removed from the ABCP program?

When the Soldier meets body fat standard; Soldiers who become pregnant while in the ABCP program will not be removed from the program

What action will be taken if a pregnant Soldier exceeds the body fat standard afte the exemption period ends?

Pending approval of a medical doctor, they will be enrolled in the ABCP

What action is taken if a Soldier becomes pregnant while enrolled in the ABCP?

No action taken. The Soldier will be flagged and enrolled in the ABCP

What action is taken for a Soldier who was hospitalized for 35 days and completes a recovery period of 90 days and fails to meet body fat standards?

The Soldier will be flagged and enrolled in the ABCP

When do the procedures of AR 600-9 apply to a Soldier hospitalized for 30 continuous days or more?

The Soldier is exempt from the program for the duration of the hospital stay and the recovery time, not to exceed 90 days. So, the procedures of AR 600-9 will apply no later than the 91st day of recovery, but could occur sooner based on medical profile and approval of medical authority

A Soldier is taking medication which causes weight gain and an inability to perform some aerobic events. What would most likely happen if the Soldier applied for an exception to policy with regards to body fat standards of AR 600-9?

The request would most likley be denied as medication, while a possible contributor to weight gain, is not sufficient justification

Explain the exception to policy process

All requests for exceptional policy will include an endorsement from a medical professional and be proceseed through the chain of command with recommendations from the company, battalion, and brigade level commanders reviewed by the JAG and submitted to the Deputy Chief of Staff G-1

A Soldier is on orders to PCS and is in the ABCP. The Soldier must extend for 8 months to meet the minimum tour requirements. What are the reenlistment procedures for Soldiers in the ABCP?

Soldiers in the ABCP will not be allowed to reenlist or extend their enlistment. Note in rare cases the General Courts Martial Convening Authority or 1st General Officer in the chain of command can make an exception to policy

Are there any exceptions to the reenlistment and/or extension rule? If so, what are they?

Yes, extensions and/or reenlistment exceptions are authorized for Soldiers who have a temporary medical condition that directly precludes loss of weight or body fat, and pregnant Soldiers who are otherwise fully qualified for reenlistment including those who approved exception to policy, but who exceed acceptable standards perscribed in AR 600-9

Who is the approving authority for exceptions to reenlistment and/or extensions?

The commander exercising general court-martial convening authority or the first general officer in the Soldier's normal chain of command

If a Soldier has 18 years of Active Federal Service, is in the ABCP, and must reenlist or extend to meet the 20-year retirement requirement, what action is taken?

If otherwise eligible, the Soldier will be extended for the minimum time required to meet 20 years. Retirement must be accomplished no later than the last day of the month in which the Soldier attains retirement eligibility

How are tape measures calibrated?

It will be compared with yardsticks or metal rulers to ensure validity

What are the maximum allowable percent of body fat for the age group 17-20?

Male = 20%; Female = 30%

What are the maximum allowable percent of body fat for the age group 21-27?

Male = 22%; Female = 32%

What are the maximum allowable percent of body fat for the age group 28-39?

Male = 24%; Female = 34%

What are the maximum allowable percent of body fat for the age group 40 and older?

Male = 26%; Female = 36%

Name two of the four eating disorders

1. Anorexia nervosa
2. Bulimia nervosa
3. Binge eating
4. Eating disorders not otherwise specified

What eating disorder is characterized by self-starvation and excessive weight loss?

Anorexia nervosa

What eating disorder is characterized by behaviors such as self-induced vomiting to undo or compensate for binge eating?

Bulimia nervosa

What eating disorder is characterized by loss of control of their eating habits?

Binge eating
