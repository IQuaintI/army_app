What are the signs and symptoms of immersion foot?

1. Cold, numb sweaty feet that may progress to hot with shooing pains
2. Slight sensory change for two to three days
3. Swelling, readiness, and bleeding may become pale and blue
4. Aches and increase pain sensitivity and infection
5. Loss of sensation
6. Severe edema and gangrene
7. Loss of tissue

What are the first aid steps for immersion foot?

1. Remove wet or constrictive clothing
2. Gently wash and dry affected extremities
3. Elevate affected limbs and cover with layers of loose, warm, and dry
4. Do NOT pop blisters, apply lotion
5. Expose to heat, permit Soldiers to walk
6. Seek medical attention
7. Evacuate
