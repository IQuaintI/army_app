How long should the ATNAA neve agent injector be held in place?

At least 10 seconds

How many ATNAA injectors can be used when administering aid for a nerve agent poisoning?

No more than three

When administering aid for nerve agent poisoning and you must give the casualty their injection of ATNAA, what else must be done?

You must administer the CANA

When a casualty has mild neve agent poisoning, should the convulsant antidote for nerve agent (CANA) be administered?

No, the CANA should not be administered

Once a nerve agent injector has been used, what should you do with it?

Bend the needle against a hard surface and attach to the blouse pocket flap

What are the signs and symptoms of severe nerve agent poisoning?

1. Strange or confused behavior
2. wheezing, difficulty breathing, coughing
3. Severely pinpointed pupils
4. Red eyes with tears
5. Vomiting
6. Severe muscle twitching
7. Involuntary urination and/or defecation

What are the two categories of nerve agent poisoning?

Mild and severe

What are three signs and symptoms of mild nerve agent poisoning?

1. Unexplained runny nose
2. Unexplained sudden headache
3. Sudden drooling
4. Tightness in chest or difficulty breathing
5. Difficulty sneezing
6. Localized sweating and muscular twitching in the area of contaminated skin, stomach cramps, and nausea

When injecting the ATNAA antidote for nerve agent, you administer the injection to the upper outer quarter of the buttock. Why is this important?

Failing to inject in the proper location increases the chance you may hit a major nerve causing paralysis

After giving yourself an initial ATNAA injection for nerve agent, should you administer a second injection?

No, do not give a second injection. If you are able to walk without assistance and know who you are and where you are, a second injection is not needed. Another Soldier should evaluate your symptoms

If you have mild nerve agent poisoning, what action should you take?

Self-administer one ANTNNA injector
