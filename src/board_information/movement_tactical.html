With regards to tactical movement techniques, when should a Soldier use the low crawl?

When crossing places where the cover and/or concealment are very low and enemy fire or observation prevents the Soldier from getting into a higher position as in high crawl or standing

With regards to tactical movement techniques, what is the advantage of using the high crawl?

Allows you to move faster than the low crawl and still gives the Soldier visibility while still maintaining a low silhouette

With regards to tactical movement techniques, when using the "RUSH" technique of movement, how long should you be exposed?

Three to five seconds

With regards to tactical movement techniques, an aerial flare appears, what should you do to protect your vision

Close one eye while the flare is burning

With regards to tactical movement techniques, name three tactical movement techniques

1.Traveling
2. Traveling overwatch
3. Bounding overwatch

When crossing any trails, what two key elements should you look for in a crossing spot?

Cover and concealment

What does the acronym SLLS stand for?

Stop, Look, Listen, Smell (These are things that should be done before moving to a new position)

When moving through tall grass, it is a good idea to do what?

Change directions

When wildlife is alerted, what should you do?

SLLS as there could be opposition nearby

If Soldiers are moving with an armored vehicle, when are Soldiers allowed to ride on the exterior of the vehicle?

When the Soldier and the vehicle are moving in the same direction and enemy contact is not likely
