Domestic Violence (AR 600-20)

Home

What is the Lautenberg Amendment?

A legislative act that makes it unlawful for any person to transfer, issue, sell or otherwise dispose of firearms or ammunition to any person whom they know or has reasonable cause to believe the individual has been convicted of the misdemeanor crime of domestic violence

What action must a Soldier take if they obtain a qualifying conviction for domestic violence?

They MUST notify their commander or supervisor of the conviction

How long may a Soldier extend their term of service to have a qualifying act of domestic violence expunged or pardoned before exiting the service?

Soldiers may extend up to one year

Soldiers with qualifying domestic violence conditions are eligible to reenlist under what conditions?

They are not allowed to reenlist. No waivers will be approved and they are not eligible for the indefinite reenlistment program

One a Soldier is convicted of a qualifying domestic violence act what is their deployable status?

They are non-deployable as they cannot carry a weapon or be around ammunition

What action will a commander take if a Soldier has a security clearance and an investigation reveals the Soldier has a qualifying misdemeanor conviction for domestic violence?

The commander will ensure the information is reported to the security manager for an incident report

What action will a commander take upon notification that a Soldier has a qualifying conviction of domestic violence?

The commander will immedietlely retrieve all government issued firearms and ammunition and advise the Soldier to consult with an attorney for guidance on the lawful disposal or sale of any privately owned firearms or ammunition

How long may a Soldier with a qualifying domestic violence conviction be allowd to remain in service?

Up to 12 months from the date HQDA is notified of the qualifying conviction

When a Soldier is convicted of a qualifying domestic violence act, when and how can they seperate from service early?

Soldiers may voluntarily request seperation for the convinience of the Government. They may also be processed for involuntary seperation under AR 635-200 if the act was a result of misconduct

A commander may not assign a Soldier convicted of a qualifying domestic violence act to what type of duty?

A position that is supervisory in nature or requires proper accountability or requires access to firearms and ammunition

What type of position or duty will a Soldier with a qualifying conviction for domestic violence be assigned?

Meaningful duties that do not require access to weapons or ammunition

Where will an extract of the AR 600-20, with regards to the Lautenberg amendment and the Gun Control Act and policy, be displayed?

Prominently displayed outside the unit arms room and all facilities in which government firearms or ammunition are stored, issued, disposed, or transported

What action will a commander take if a Soldier with a qualifying domestic violence conviction comes down on orders for an overseas assignment?

The orders will be deleted as the Solder is ineligible for overseas duty

How often will an individual with a qualifying conviction for domestic violence qualify with their assigned weapon?

They are exempt from weapon qualification and will not be assigned an individual weapon or ammunition
