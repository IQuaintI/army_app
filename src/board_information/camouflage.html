Camouflage (STP 21-1, TC 3-21.75, FM 20-3)

Home

How should camouflage sticks be applied to exposed skin?

High shiny areas with dark colors and low shiny areas with light colors

Define cover

It can be natural or manmade. It gives protection from bullets, fragments of exploding rounds, flame, nuclear effects, biological/chemical agents, and enemy observation

Define concealment

Anything that protects you from enemy observation but does not protect you from enemy fire

When camouflage paint is not available, what can be used as a substitute?

Burnt cork, bark, charcoal, lamp black, or light-colored mud

What are the three types of cover?

1. Overhead
2. Frontal
3. Flank/Rear

In order for the radar scattering capability of a camouflage net to work, how much space must be between the net and the piece of equipment covering it?

At a minimum of two feet

What are some examples of the two types of cover and concealment?

Artificial cover/concealment: Foxholes, trenches, walls, burlaps, nets, or natural material that has been moved from their original spots such as leaves or bushes
Natural cover/concealment: Ravines, hollows, reverse slopes, bushes, grass, and shadows

What do you do with the excess dirt when you are digging a fighting position?

Move it to the rear of the fighting position so it cannot be seen when approaching the position from the front and preferably the flank

When using natural material to camouflage a fighting position, what should you remember?

Do not disturb the natural look of the surrounding area and use only items that could be commonly procured in that area

What is blending?

Trying to alter a position's appearance so that it becomes a part of the background by arranging or applying camouflage materials on, over, and around a position to ensure it blends in with its surroundings

When should you change the camouflage around your position?

When it is dying or no longer blends in with its surroundings

How far away should a properly concealed position remain difficult to see?

From at least 35 meters or 115 feet

Name some examples of manmade cover
