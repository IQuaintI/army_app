Name two of the six situations when salutes are not required?

1. When one or both individuals are in civilians
2. Engaged in routine work if the salute would interfere
3. Carrying articles with both hands so occupied as to make saluting impracticable
4. Working as a member of a detail or engaged in sports or social functions where saluting would present a safety hazard
5. In public places such as theaters, churches, and in public conveyances
6. In the ranks of a formation

What honors will an active duty Soldier and Medal of Honor recipients receive at a funeral?

Name three Army customs

1. Rendering a salute
2. Rendering a respect to the flag, reveille, and retreat
3. Never criticize leaders, Soldiers, or the Army in public
4. Always use the chain of command
5. Make no excuses - take responsibility for your actions
6. Always speak with your own voice

When are cannon salutes NOT fired?

Between retreat and reveille, on Sundays, or on national holidays (excluding Memorial Day and Independence Day), unless at the discretion of the commander directing the honors, international courtesy or the occasion requires a exception

What type of salute will be fired on Independence Day?

All Army installations will fire a 50-gun salute to the Union at 1200 hours provided the installation has the necessary equipment for firing salutes

Upon the death of the President, a former President, or President elect of the United States, what action will be taken on the day of interment at all military installations that are properly equipped to provide honors?

A 21-gun salute will be fired commencing at noon. Guns will be fired one minute apart. Uniformed personnel will not salute. An additional salute of a number of guns equal to the number of States of the Union will be fired at retreat. The salute will be fired at five second intervals

Name conditions when a funeral honors will NOT be held

1. To a Soldier that is AWOL
2. An individual convicted of a capital offense in which the person was sentenced to death or life imprisonment without parole
3. Any individual convicted of serious offense (meaning if persecuted could result in confinement of six months or more, or punitive discharge)
4. To individuals who have at any time been discharged or released from military service with any of the following: dishonorable discharge or a bad conduct discharge
5. Dismissal from the service awarded by a court-martial
6. An other than honorable discharge
7. Officer resignation in lieu of court-martial in lieu of court-martial which results in an other than honorable discharge

How long will the National Flag be flown at half-staff for the death of a President, former President, or President-Elect?
