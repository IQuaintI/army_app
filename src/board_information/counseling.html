According to AR 635-200, what must be done before initiating seperation proceedings against a Soldier?

Diligent efforts will be made to identify Soldiers who exhibit a likelihood for early seperation and to improve their chances for retention through counseling, retraining, and rehabilitation prior to intitation of seperation proceedings

According to AR 635-200, can adverse information from a previous period of service be used when deciding to seperate a Soldier?

Yes, adverse information from a prior enlistment or period of service only when such information would have a direct and strong prohibative value in determining wheather seperation is appropriate

According to AR 635-200, name three factors that should be considered when deciding whether to seperate a Soldier?

1. Seriousness of the event
2. Effect of the Soldier's continued retention on military discipline, good order, and morale
3. Likelihood that the events or conditions will continue or recur
4. Likelihood the Soldier will be disruptive or an undesireable influence in present or future duty sessions
5. Soldier's ability to perform duties effectively now and in the future including potential for advancement/leadership
6. Soldier's rehabilitative potential
7. Soldier's entire military record, past contributions to the Army, assignments, awards, decorations, evaluation ratings, and letter of commendation
8. Memoranda of reprimand or admonition, counseling records, records of nonjudicial punishment, records of conviction bu court-martial and records of involvement with civilian authorities
9. Other matters deemed relevant by the separation authority including specialized training, duties and experience
10. Adverse information from a prior enlistment or period of service only when such information would have a direct and strong probative value in determining whether seperation is appropriate
