What actions can a commander take if a Soldier is participating in extremist activity?

They can consider punishment under Articles 92, 116, 117, or 134 of UCMJ. They can pursue involuntary seperation , bar to reenlistment, and/or other administrative/ disciplinary actions

Give two examples that the chain of command can use to determine participation in criminal gangs?

1. Wearing of gang colors or clothing, tattoos or body markings associated with criminal gangs
2. Slogan
3. Grafitti
4. Clothing style or color
5. Activities furthering the objectives of gang organizations that are detrimental to good order
6.Discipline that is incompatible with military service

What administrative action can the command take if a Soldier is participating in extremist activity?

1. Command will advise a Soldier that participation in extremist organizations or activities will be taken into consideration when evaluating their overall duty performance, to include appropriate remarks on evaluation reports
2. Involvment may result in removal of security clearance, reclassification, removal/bar from ledership positions, or bar to reenlistment
3. Actions can be reported to law enforcement

How can an individual report someone for extremist activity?

It can be reported to the chain of command or made by anonymous call
