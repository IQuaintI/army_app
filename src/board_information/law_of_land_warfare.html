They will be treated as a POW pending determination of their status by a competent tribunal

Treaties and customary international law

Describe a flag of truce

A white flag

What does a flag of truce indicate?

It only indicates a desire to communicate with the enemy and has no other significance

When can a flag of truce be used as a feign or used to gain information for an attack?

It can never be used for these purposes

Under what condition can the Red Cross be used to protect a military operation?

They can only be used to identify persons engaged in humanitarian work. They may not be used in any way to protect a military operation

What are the three classes of lawful combatants?

1. Members of the armed forces
2. Members of militia or volunteer corps
3. Inhabitants of an area who participate in a popular uprising to defend against foreign invaders

Who as the right to status as a prisoner of war?

Any lawful combatant

What acts can cause an individual to be labeled as an unprivileged belligerent?

Spying, sabatoge, or civilians joining armed armed groups that do not qualify as lawful combatants

How can an unprivileged belligerent be punishment?

By trial and punishment of a military tribunal

You have been involved in a firefight and the enemy executed members of your unit. You are about to counter-attack. In retaliation for the execution of your Soldiers you will give the following order "No quarter or surrender will be accepted from the enemy. All prisoners will be executed." Is this order lawful?

This is not a legal order. You may not execute individuals that surrender nor can you give such an order. It is illegal to make statements like "there will be no survivors" or threaten the enemy that you will deny their surrender

Name four things that apply to executing your duties as a Soldier under the Law of Land Warfare?

1. Fight only the enemy
2. Do not harm enemies who surrender
3. Disarm the enemy and turn them over to your supervisors
4. Do not kill or torture enemy prisoners of war or other detainees
5. Collect and care for the wounded, wheather friend or foe
6. Do not attack medical personnel or medical facilities or medical equipment
7. Destroy no more than the mission requires
8. Treat all civilians humanely
9. Do not steal. Respect the property of others
10. Do your best to prevent violations of the law of war
11. Report all violations of the law of war to your supervisors

What is the main purpose of the Law of Armed Conflict?

1. Protecting combatants, non-combatants, and civilians from unnecessary suffering
2. Providing certain fundamental protections for persons who fall into the hands of the enemy, such as prisoners of war, and the wounded
3. Facilitating the restoration of peace
4. Assisting the commander in ensuring the disciplined, ethical, and effective use of military force
5. Preserving the professionalism and humanity of combatants
6. Preventing the degeneration of armed conflict into barbarism

What is the definition of an international armed conflict (IAC)?

Any declared war between States, or to any other armed conflict between States, even if the state of war is not recognized by one of them

What is the definition of a non-international armed conflict (NIAC)?

It is an armed conflict NOT between States, such as a conflict between a State and a non-State armed group or a conflict between two non-State armed groups

Define military necessity

It justifies the use of all measures required to defeat the enemy as quickly and efficiently as possible that are not prohibited by the law of armed conflict

Define Humanity

Basis of protection for civilians, forbids inflicting suffering, injury, damage, or destruction unnecessary to accomplish a legitimate military purpose

Define Honor

Demands a certain amount of fairness and a certain mutual respect between opposing forces

Define Distinction

Distinguishing between combatants and military objectives on the one hand and civilian objects on the other inn offense and defense

Define Proportionality

Requires commanders to refrain from attacks in which the expected loss or injury to civilian and their property would be excessive in relation to the concrete and direct military advantage to be gained. It also underlines preventative measures to be taken to minimize harm to civilians

Name the Law of Armed Conflict principles

1. Military necessity
2. Humanity
3. Honor
4. Distinction
5. Proportionality

How should Soldiers treat and observe current treaties the U.S recognizes?

As if they were federal law

When do LOAC rules cease to apply?

When the hostilities

When identifying targets what must be taken into consideration?

The impact that it may have on the civilian population with regards to wounding or killing them as a result of collateral damage

What action can be taken against individuals who parachute from an aircraft in distress?

No action can be taken. They cannot be made an object of attack

Name three persons or objects that are not military objectives and are protected from being made the object of attack so long as those protections have not been forfeited?

1. Individual civilians and the civilian population
2. Military medical personnel and chaplains
3. Individuals who are hors de combat
4. Civilian objects or other protected objects, that is, all objects that are not military objectives
5. Medical units, transport, and equipment
6. Undefended villages, towns, and cities

What action can be taken against a persons parachuting into combat?

They may be attacked throughout their descent and upon landing

What action is required when a flag of truce is displayed?

No action is required as the opposing force is not required to cease fire merely because a flag of truce has been displayed

When is pillage prohibited?

It is always prohibited to pillage and specifically with respect to the military wounded, sick, and shipwrecked

Define Pillage

The taking of private or public movable property, including enemy military equipment, for private or personal use

How must all detainees be treated?

They must be treated humanely and protected against cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment or punishment

What action will be taken for any serious injury or death of a POW?

There will be an official investigation to determine the cause of the injury or death

Name four types of weapons that are prohibited?

1. Poison, posioned weapons, posionous gases, and other chemical weapons
2. Biological weapons
3. Certain enviromental modification techniques
4. Weapons that injure by fragmentation that are non-detectable by x-rays
5. Certain types of mines, booby traps, and other devices
6. Lasers specifically designed to cause permanent blindness to unenhanced vision

POW's may be employed in what type of labor activities?

POW's can work at labors that place them at risk of violence, intimidation, insults, or public curiosity, or what would be regarded as humiliating by members of the detaining military force. Additionally, unless they volunteer, they cannot be made to perform labor that is dangerous or unhealthy. They may not be compelled to take part in military operations directed against their own forces

How many times can a POW be punished for the same act or change?

Only once
