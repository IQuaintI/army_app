Name three cold-weather injuries

1. Hypothermia
2. Frostbite
3. Frostnip
4. Snow blindness

What is hypothermia?

It s defined as a body core temperature below 95 degrees Farenheit

What are the signs and symptoms of hypothermia?

1. Vigorous shivering
2. Shivering may decrease or cease as temperature falls
3. Conscious but usually apathetic or lethargic
4. Confusion
5. Sleepiness
6. Slurred speech
7. Shallow breathing
8. Very slow respiration
9. Weak pulse
10. Low or unattainable blood pressure
11. Change in behavior with or without poor control over body movement
12. Uncordinated movement, shock, and comma may occur as temperature drops
13. Skin may be pale, cold, and clammy

What are the first aid procedures for hypothermia?

Mild Hypothermia:

Warm body evenly without delay (provide a heat source), keep dry, protect from elements, warm liquids may be given only to a conscious casualty, be prepared to give CPR if necessary, and seek medical attention immediately

Severe Hypothermia:

Quickly stabilize body temp, attempt to prevent further heat loss, handle gently, and seek medical attention immediately

What is required to properly diagnose hypothermia?

A rectal core temperature must be taken

What is frostbite?

The freezing of skin

What is the color of frostbite skin?

First it turns red and then becomes pale grey or waxy white

What are the signs and symptoms of frostbite?

1. Numbness in affected areas
2. Blistered, swollen, and tender areas
3. Pale, yellow, or waxy skin (greyish in dark-skinned Soldiers)
4. Frozen tissue that feels wooden to touch
5. Significant pain after rewarming
6. Tingling, stinging, aching sensation, and cramping pain

What are the first aid measures for frostbite?

1. Rewarming at room temperature or using body heat
2. Loosen or remove constricting clothing and remove jewelry
3. Move casualty to a sheltered area
4. Protect affected areas from further exposure to cold
5. Once thawed do not allow the affected area to refreeze
6. Avoid exposure to excessive heat
7. Seek medical attention immediately
8.Evacuate casualty to a medical facility

What are the two classifications of frostbite?

Superficial and deep

When should frostbite be thawed?

Only when a Soldier will be moved to an area in which the possibilty of refreezing is no longer present
