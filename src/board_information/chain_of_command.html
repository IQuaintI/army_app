Define Chain of Command

The sequence of commanders in an organization who have direct authority and primary responsibilty for accomplishing the assigned unit mission while caring for personnel and property in their charge

When can an enlisted person assume command?

In the absence of all officers and pending the arrival of an officer the Senior NCO assigned to the unit will exercise temporary command

What does AR 600-20 state with regards to using the chain of command?

Soldiers WILL use the chain of command when communicating issues and problems to their leaders and commanders

What regulation covers Army Command Policy and Procedures?

AR 600-20

Can commanders delegate authority and responsibilty to a subordinate? If so, explain?

Commanders are responsible for everything their command does or fails to do. However, commanders subdivide responsibilty and authority and assign portions of both to various subordinate commanders and staff members. In this way, proper degree of responsibilty becomes inherent in each command echelon. Commanders delegate sufficient authority to Soldiers in the chain of command to accomplish their assigned duties, and commanders may hold these Soldiers responsible for their actions. Commanders who assign responsibilty and authority to their subordinates still retain overall responsibilty for the actions of their command

What is emergency command?

When the senior military member on the scene of an emergency assumes temporary command and control of the Soldiers present at the scene

An enlisted member in temporary command of a unit is not authorized, except in urgent cases, to alter, void, or change ___

Standing orders of the permanent commander without authority from the next higher commander

NCOs will only participate in work details in a supervisory capacity except when?

Temporary shortage of personnel requires the NCO to actively participate in the work detail

How is seniority determined among enlisted members when they are of the same rank?

First by date of rank. When rank of service is the same then by total length of active federal service. If both date of rank and active federal service are the same then seniority will be determined by date of birth
