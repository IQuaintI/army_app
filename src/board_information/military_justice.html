When is the use of non-judicial punishment appropriate?

In all cases involving minor offenses in which non-punitive measures are considered inadequate or inappropriate

What are non-punitive measures?

Those actions taken by leaders to correct minor infractions that do not rise to the level of judicial punishment. For example: A Soldier would be given corrective training as a non-punitive measure to correct substandard performance

What is the purpose of nonjudicial punishment?

1. Correct
2. Educate
3. Reform offenders whom the imposing commander determines cannot benefit from less stringent measures
4. Preserve a Soldiers record from the stigma of a court-martial
5. Further efficiency by disposing of minor offenses in a manner requiring less time and personnel than trial by court-martial

What type of conduct do non-punitive measaures usually deal with?

Misconduct resulting from: simple neglect, forgetfulness, laziness, inattention to instructions, sloppy habits, immaturity, difficulty adjusting to military life and similar deficiencies

Do non-punitive measures constitute punishment?

No, non-punitive measures do not constitute punishment. They are tools for teaching proper standards of conduct and p0erformance

Give three examples of non-punitive measures?

1. Denial of pass or other privileges
2. Counseling
3. Administrative reduction in grade
4. Administrative reprimands and admonitions
5. Extra training
6. Bar to reenlistment
7. MOS reclassification

What are the rules pertaining to extra training, extra instruction, and corrective training?

1. Must be directly related to the offense
2. Must be oriented to correct the deficiency
3. May be conducted after normal duty hours
4. Cannot be punitive in nature
5. Will be discontinued when the deficiency is corrected

Where will an Article 15 be filed for a Soldier found guilty of a sex related offense?

It must be filed in the performance file. No sex related offense can be filed in the restricted file

What is the maximum punishment that may be imposed under the summary proceeding of an Article 15?

1. 14 days of extra duty
2. Restriction for 14 days
3. Oral reprimand or admonition
4. Any combination of the above

What action must be taken when a Soldier is being considered for a Summary Article 15 and does not initially demand trial by court-martial, then during the sentencing phase the Soldier demands a trial by court-martial?

1. No action is required
2. The Soldier must demand a trial by court-martial at the start of the hearing
3. The decision is irrevocable

How long does a Soldier have to submit an appeal when receiving a Summary Article 15?

Normally five calender days

Is a Soldier entitled to a copy of a Summary Article 15?

Yes, if a Soldier's request is submitted during the filing period

Under the formal proceedings of an Article 15, does the Soldier have the right to legal counsel?

Yes, if so requested

Under the formal proceedings of an Article 15, how long does a Soldier have to decide if they will demand trail by court martial? Are they allowed to seek legal counsel during duty hours?

A Soldier will normally be given 48 hours to seek legal counsel and determine if they want trial by court-martial. They will also be allowed time off work to seek legal counsel

Once a trial by court-martial is requested, can the Soldier withdraw the demand?

Yes, the Soldier can voluntarily withdraw the request for trial by court-martial

Define an open hearing under the proceedings of an Article 15

A Soldier may request an open hearing in which members of the public may attend. However, commanders are not required to hold the proceedings in a location different from that in which the commander conducts normal business

Define a closed hearing under the proceedings of an Article 15

A Soldier may request a closed hearing, one in which the commander decides that members of the public will not attend

When can civilian counsel be present during an Article 15 proceeding?

The Soldier may retain civilian counsel to act as the Soldier's spokesperson, at the Solder's expense

Commanders are the ultimate authority when imposing punishment under Article 15 proceedings. However, they are encouraged to consult certain individuals prior to imposing punishment. Who are these people?

NCOs should be consulted on the appropriate type, duration, and limits of punishment to be imposed. However, the commander makes the final decision

What is the maximum punishment that can be imposed during a company grade Article 15?

1. 14 days extra duty
2. 14 days restriction
3. 7 days correctional custody
4. 3 days diet confinement (on board a vessel for E-1 thru E-3)
5. Reduction in grade by 1 grade for E-1 thru E-4
6. Forfeiture of 7 days pay

What is the maximum punishment that can be imposed during a field grade Article 15?

1. 45 days of extra duty
2. 60 days restriction
3. 30 days correctional custody
4. 4 days diet confinement (for E-1 thru E-3)
5. Reduction in grade by one or more grades for E-1 thru E-4, reduction of 1 grade for NCO during peace time
6. Forfeiture of half months pay for two months

On what date does imposed punishments begin unless otherwise directed by the commander?

On the date the punishment is imposed

Once the punishment is imposed or after an appeal decision is made, what action may the commander take?

The punishment may be announced at the next unit formation and/or the Article 15 may be posted on the unit bulletin board

How long can punishments be suspended for company and field grade Article 15s

No more than six months

How long can punishment be suspended for a summarized article 15?

No more than three months

If an appeal is not made in a timely matter, what action may be taken by the appeal authority?

The appeal may be rejected as untimely and no action will be taken

Where are company grade and field grade Article 15s filed for NCO's?

The commander will place the Article 15 in either the performance or restricted file of the Soldier military personnel file

Where are company grade and field grade Article 15s filed for SPC and below?

It will be filed locally for a period of two years or until the Soldier is transferred to another general court-martial convening authority, unless the Article 15 was the result of a sex related offense, then the document will be filed in the Soldier's performance file

What is the purpose of an Article 138 complaint?

It is a formal complaint filed against a commanding officer

What action must a Soldier take before submitting an Article 138 complaint?

The Soldier must first attempt to solve the issue by submitting a written and signed Initial Request for Redress. In addition, an electronic message will also satisfy this requirement

How long does a commander have to respond to a Soldier's Initial Request for Redress?

The response must be within 15 days. It must be written and an electronic message will be enough to satisfy this request

What action must a commander take if they cannot provide a final response to a Soldier's Initial Request for Redress within 15 days?

The commander will provide an interim response that estimates the date of the final response

What can a Soldier infer if they do not recieve either an interim response or final response from the commander?>

The Soldier can infer or elect to treat the lack of response as a refusal of redress

How long does a Soldier have to submit an Article 138 complaint from the time of the wrong?

90 days from the time of the wrong

Under what conditions can an Article 138 be withdrawn by the Soldier?

It can be withdrawn anytime before the final action is taken. This action must be voluntary on the part of the complaining Soldier

Who ultimately recieves the Article 138 for action?

The general court-martial convening authority. The authority may not be delegated

What action must the General Court-Martial Convening Authority take upon the final decision of the Article 138 complaint

They must notify the complaining Soldier in writing of the action taken

Define admonition

A warning or reminder given to an offender to refrain or deter a certain type of miscondct and to advise the offender of the consequences that may follow for another episode of that misconduct

Define reprimand

An act of formal censure the reproves or rebukes an offender for misconduct

What are the two types and methods thar a reprimand or admonition can be given?

They can be written or oral. They can be imposed as an administrative measure or as a non-judicial punishment

Is extra training or extra instruction a punitive or non-punitive action?

It is non-punitive action

If a Soldier has already recieved extra training, extra instruction, or corrective training, what action should a commander take?

A commander should not take action under non-judicial punishment for offenses in which the Soldier has already recieved corrective or extra training

A senior commander has issued an order that they reserve the right to handle any offense dealing with DUI or DWI, thus preventing a subordinate commander from taking any action in this situation. It this legal?

THis action is legal and supported under paragraph 3-4 of AR 27-10. A superior commander may reserve the right to exercise UCMJ authority over certain types of offenses or categories of offenses

At what level should UCMJ proceedings be administered?

At the lowest level of command appropriate for dealing with the offense

A Soldier is being considered for punishment under an Article 15 proceeding. Before administering punishment, what facts and/or conditions should a commander thoroughly consider before administering punishment?

The nature and circumstances of the offense, the age, the record, the maturity, and the experience of the offender

When a commander is making the determination on where to file an Article 15 (in the performance or restricted file) what shuold the commander consider?

The Soldier's age, grade, total service (with particular attention to the Soldier's recent performance and past miscondct), and whether the Soldier has more than one record of nonjudicial punishment directed for filing in the restricted folder

Who determines if the offense is "minor" and should be handled under the proceedings of an Article 15?

The commander

A Soldier is being considered for an Article 15 for disrespect to an officer. While the paperwork is being processed for an Article 15, the Soldier is found out to have lied to an NCO about their performance. THe command recommends the Soldier for a second Article 15. Is this legal?

No. When a Soldier is being considered for punishment under Article 15 all incidents at the time will be disposed of under one Article 15 proceeding?

What is the statue of limitation for the imposition of punishment under the proceeding of an Article 15?

Non-judicial punishment may not be imposed for any offense that was committed more than two years before the date of imposition

While embarked or attached to a vessel, when is a Soldier permitted to request a trial by court martial?

A Soldier aboard a vessel is not permitted to demand a trial by court-martial

During a summary Article 15 proceeding, the Soldier is indecisive about requesting an Article 15. Wat can the Soldier do about the situation?

The Soldier may request a reasonable amount of time, normally 24 hours, to decide if the want a trial by court-martial

When a Soldier submits an appeal, they are required to perform all imposed punishment while the appeal is being considered. If a decision on the appeal has not been made within three days of the appeal, what actions can the Soldier take?

The Soldier may request that any punishment involving the deprivation of liberty be suspended or delayed until the decision is made on their appeal

What is the time limit the command has to decide to take action on an appeal under summary Article 15 proceedings?

Three calender days

Who can notify the Soldier of the commander's intent to use Article 15 proceedings?

The commander or his designated representative which must be a comissioned officer or an NCO in the grade of SFC or above, provided the person doing the notification is senior to the Soldier being notified

When is a Soldier provided or allowed to have legal representation during an Article 15 proceeding?

A Soldier has no right to legal representation during the execution of an Article 15

What unique punishment can be imposed on a Soldier embarked or attached to a vessel?

Confinement on bread and water or diminished rations for a PFC or below

There are restrictions as to what extra duty cannot include. WHat are the restrictions?

1. Extra duty cannot be cruel or unusual punishment
2. Using the offender as a personal servant
3. Duty normally intended to be used as an honor
4. The offender cannot be required to perform a task that is ridiculous or degrading
5. Any task that is a safety or health hazard
6. Any task that would demean the Soldier's position as an NCO or specialist

If a Soldier starts 14 days of restriction under an Article 15 and the commander grants leave to the Soldier for five days, what is the impact on the punishment?

The punishment must run consecutively. This means that those five days would count towards those 14 days. The Soldier would serve the remaining nine days

What action must be taken beofre an Article 15 can be posted to the unit board?

All private information must be removed

What is the purpose of posting and announcing the results of a punishment?

To preclude perceptions of unfairness and to deter similar misconduct by other Soldiers

What action will the superior take if a Soldier initially decides not to appeal an Article 15 and later decides to do so within he five-day period?

The superior may reject the subsequent election to appeal

When is an Article 138 complaint appropriate?

If upon application to the commanding officer who supposedly wronged the Soldier is refused address, the Soldier may complain to any superior officer, who shall forward the complaint to the office exercising court-martial jurisdiction over the officer against whom the complaint is being made

When is an Article 138 complaint inappropriate?

If another course of redress is available to the Soldier, an Article 138 is inappropriate

If a reprimand or admonition is imposed under non-punitive mesures, it must contain what type of statement?

It must contain a statement that it was imposed as an administrative measure and not as punishment under UCMJ Article 15

A senior commander has issued an order to a subordinate commander directing certain offenses be punished under UCMJ Article 15 and that these offenses will recieve a specific type of punishment. Is this order supported?

No article of the UCMJ supports this order. This order is illegal as no senior commander can direct a subordinate commander to deal with an offense using Article 15 proceedings and they cannot direct a specific punishment

Two Soldiers have committed the same offense. Should both Soldiers recieve the same punishment under Article 15 proceedings?

It depends. The commander should consider the nature and circumstances of the offense, the age, previous record, maturity, and experience of the offender. If after careful consideration the commander may decide that different punishments may be appropriate for each offender

A commander imposing an Article 15 may choose to file the Article 15 in the Soldier's performance file or in the Soldier's restricted file. Why is this decision so important?

Filing the Article 15 in the performance file will potentially have a significant impact on the Soldier's career as it could end their career or prevent promotion, but filing the Article 15 in the restricted file will not have this harsh of an impact

Under what conditions should an Article 15 be filed in a Soldier's performance file?

When the facts show unmitigated moral turpitude or lack of integrity, patterns of misconduct, evidence of serious character deficiency, or a substantial breach of military discipline

What standard is used to determine if an offense is "minor" in nature and should be handled under the proceedings of an Article 15?

Only those offenses that would be considered "average" and punishable by the summary court-martial. If the offense would be tried by a court-martial, above a summary court-martial, then the offenses should not be disposed of under the proceedings of an Article 15

Does the imposition of an Article 15 prevent a Soldier from being tried by court-martial for the same offense?

No, the Soldier can still be tried by court-martial for the same offense, however the accused can show that the issue was already disposed of under an Article 15 and this fact "must" be considered

How is the statute of limitation for imposing punishment under the proceeding of an Article 15 impacted when a Soldier is AWOL?

When a SOldier is AWOL, fleeing justice, outside the United States ability to apprehend, in the custody of civil authorities or in he hands of the enemy, the statute of limitations time stops. Therefore, the time does not restart until the Soldier is within duty status

During the proceedings of a summary Article 15, the Soldier requests an appointment with a JAG. How long does the Soldier have to make an appointment with the JAG office?

The Soldier has no right to counsel under the proceeding of a summary Article 15

What are the filing requirements of a summary Article 15?

They will be filled locally and destroyed at the end of two years from the date of punishment or destroyed when the Soldier is transferred out of the unit

WHen a commander has the intent to initiate Article 15 proceedings, what action must be taken

The Soldier must be notified of the commander's intent to use Article 15 proceedings. Soldiers will be notified of the maximum punishment the commander could impose and the Soldier will be provided a copy of the DA Form 2627 with items 1 and 2 complete, including date and signature of the imposing commander

What action will a commander take if a Soldier has demanded trial by court-martial but refuses to complete or sign the DA Form 2627 within the prescribed time?

The commander will inform the SOldier that failure to complete or sign item 3 may be treated as a voluntary withdrawal of any oral demand for trial by court-martial

WHen performing extra duty, the Soldier is directed to perform as a member of a color guard or funeral detail as part of the extra duty. Is this legal?

It is not legal as AR 27-10 specifically states extra duty will not allow duty normaly intended as an honor such as assignment to a guard of honor

How is the amount of forfeiture of pay imposed under Article 15 proceedings rounded?

To the next lower whole dollar

Whenan Article 15 punishment involves deprivation of liberty it must run continuously without interruption. WHat are the exceptions to this rule?

When the punishment is interrupted due to the fault of the Soldier such as the Soldier being physically incapacitated or an appeal is not acted upon within three calender days

How does the Soldier appeal a commanders decision to vacae a suspended punishment?

There is no appeal process

Define mitigation

The reduction in either the quantity or quality of a punishment

Define remission

An action in which any portion of the unexecuted punishment is cancelled

Define "Wholly set aside"

An action in which all punishment imposed upon an individual under the Article 15 restores or "sets aside" all rights, privileges, or property affected. This decisions tends to be taken during a clear injustice

Define a "clear injustice"

It means that an unaided legal or factual error that clearly and affirmatively injured the substantial rights of the Soldier

If a Soldier is a military sex offender, what action will they take when arriving at a duty station?

They must register with the installation Provost Marshal and with state and local officials

What action may be taken against a military sex offender that fails to properly register themselves with the appropriate officials?

They may be subject to criminal prosecution under state law and under UCMJ Article 92 for failure to obey the requirements of AR 27-10

How long does a military sex offender have to report address changes as provided by law to the installation Provost Marshal

They must report the change within 5 calender days
