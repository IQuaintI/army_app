When can a relief for cause action be taken?

When a commander loses confidence in a subordinate's ability to command due to misconduct, poor judgement, inability to complete assigned duties, or similar reasons

In most cases, what is required prior to coniducting a relief for cuase?

Counseling unless it is deemed inapproriate or not practical under the circumstances

What regulation covers the evaluation reporting system?

AR 623-3

How will the rating chain be structured?

They will correspond with the chain of command or chains of supervision

When can retroactive changes be made to the rating scheme?

Never

What support forms will a Soldier recieve at the beginning of the rated period?

A copy of the rater's and senior rater's suppor form

When will an initial NCOER counseling be conducted?

Initially within 30 days of the start of the rating period and quarterly thereafter

Who will normally be appointed a Soldier's rater?

Their immediate supervisor

How is pooling used in the evaluation system?

Pooling is illegal and is not to be used in the evaluation system

How many days of obvservation must a Senior Rater have to rate Soldier?

A minimum of 60 calender days

What document tells a rated Soldier who their supplementary reviewer is?

The published rating scheme

What is the normal minimum rating period for an evaluation period?

90 days

WHat form is used to document the initial and quarterly counseling sessions for an NCOER?

DA Form 2166-9-1A

What action may the reviewer take when there are discrepancies in the evaluation report that they believe are not consistent with the evaluation process?

They can attach a memorandum as an external attachment for submission to HQDA

What are the two types of evaluation reports?

Mandatory and optional

1. Safety
2. Individual readiness
3. Energy informed actions
4. Support of behavioral health goals
5. Support of EEO
6. Suppot of equal opportunity
7. Support of SHARP programs

What information will the rated Soldier provide to the rater after the initial counseling session?

The rated Soldier is responsible for drafting performance goals and expectations and providing major performance objectives. They should use the rater and senior rater support forms to help them accomplish this goal

What documents does the rated Soldier use when developing goals and objectives?

The rater and senior rater support forums

How will the rated NCO verify the face-to-face counseling sessions with the rater?

By dating and initialing the support form or the DA Form 2166-9-1A

Who maintains a personal working copy of the NCO support form?

The rated NCO

How often will follow-up counseling be conducted?

Quarterly

An NCO will never complete their own evaluation report however they may supply the rater with what information?

They may supply a list of their achievments and accomplishments for the rated period

How is performance evaluated by the rater?

The rater will consider the results achieved, how they were achieved, and how well the NCO complied with professional standards

Who is responsible for rating the area of potential on the evaluation form?

The senior rater

Who has the overarching role of ensuring compliance with the evaulating reporting process, policy guidelines, of AR 623-3, and the procedural guidance of DA Pam 623-3?

The supplementary reviewer

If a Soldier seeks voluntary mental health counseling for a condition not detected by the chain of command what comments may be entered on the evaluation report?

None.It will not be placed on the evaluation report as this would discourage self-referral to obtain assistance from healthcare professionals

Name three of five incidents that REQUIRE comments on an evaluation report when substantiated by an Army investigation?

Substantiated findings of:
1. EO complaints
2. Findings of sexual harassment and/or assault
3. Failure to report sexual harassment and/or sexual assault
4. Failure to respond to a complaint or report of sexua harassment and/or sexual assault
5. Retaliation against a person making a complaint or report of sexual harassment and/or sexual assault

What chapter of AR 623-3 covers the appeal process?

Chapter 4

What is the purpose of the commander's inquiry?

It provides a greater degree of command involvement and prevents injustices and corrects errors before the report becomes a matter of permanent record

What action must the commander take if they believe an evaluation report might be illegal, unjust, or in violation of the evaluation regulation?

A commander's inquiry must be conducted

Who can require a change be made to an evaluation report?

No one can require changes be made to an evaluation report

Who has access to evaluation reports?

The rating chain, the rated Soldier, and those that process the report

To qualify as a rater, the rater must be assigned for a minimum of how many calender days?

90 calender days

Define additional duties

Additional responsibilities not normally associated with the duty description

What is a commanders inquiry?

An investigation into a Soldier's evaluation report made by officials in the chain of command to determine if something in the report is illegal, unjust, or in violation of regulation

How is a Soldier evaluated when they are on leave for a period of more than 30 days?

This period of time is considered non-rated time

What is a rater tendency report?

A document provided by HQDA for raters of NCOs. It shows the rater's rating assessment history by grade of previous NCOs rated

What is redress?

The process by which a Soldier can address errors, bias, or injustices during and after the preparation of an evaluation report and have them corrected

Define relief

The removal of a Soldier from a position due to loss of confidence based on the Soldiers personal or professional conduct, behavior or performance. Requires a Relief for Cause report

What action must take place if there is no uniformed Army designated official in the rating scheme?

A supplementary review must be conducted

When necessary who does the senior rater foward the evaluation report to for review?

The supplementary reviewer

Name three of the seven responsibilities of the senior rater

1. Ensure evaluation chains are correct
2. Ensure any discrepanciesare resolved
3. Ensure the report complies with the regulation
4. Ensure the communication process between the rater and rated Soldier has taken place and is documented properly
5. Proper counseling has taken place
6. Rated Soldier recieved a copy of the final evaluation
7. Ensure all reports are submitted to HQDA

If the rater failed to conduct proper counseling what action must be taken?

The senior rater must address the lack of counsling in part V, block B, explaining why counseling was not conducted

Who conducts the review for a "Relief for Cause" report?

The senior rater provided they are a SGM/CSM, CPT, or above

What action will the senior rater take if they find inconsistencies or unclear comments in the evaluation report?

The senior rater will return the evaluagtion report to the rater indicating what is wrong. They will avoid all statements and actions that may influence or alter an honest evaluation by the rater

Who conducts an undocumented review of the evaluation report?

The 1ST, SGM, or CSM

What action is taken with regards to an evaluation report for isolated minor incidents in a Soldier's performance?

The evaluation will not normally be based on these incidents

What is the purpose of the DA Form 2166-9-1A?

This form assures a verified communication process for the rating period

What form accompanies the final draft of the NCOER when fowarded to the senior rater?

The NCOER support form or DA Form 2166-9-1A

Can the failure to use a support form or failure to counsel be the basis for appeal?

Yes, it can be a basis for appeal, but it cannot be the sole reason for the appeal

If there is geographical seperation between the rated Soldier and the rater, what action can be taken for counseling?

Corresponence and telephone communications may be used as alternatives to face-to-face counseling. However face-to-face counseling will be conducted with the rated Soldier at the earliest opportunity

What items will the rater provide to the rated Soldier during the initial counseling?

1. They will provide a copy of the rater and senior rater support form
2. Discuss the unit mission
3. Provide a valid rating chain
Duty description
Specific goals and objectives

What does good counseling focus on?

Good counseling focuses on the learning that occured without dwelling on the past and the rated NCO's progression towards meeting goals and objectives

How do the follow-up counseling sessions differ from the initial counseling session?

The follow up counseling session informs the rated NCO how well they are performing, how they can perform better, serve as a time to update duty description and performance objectives

Who is responsible for obtaining the APFT (ACFT) and height/weight information fro the NCOER?

The rater

Once the counseling session is complete what action does the rater take?

The rater will initial the support form and foward it to the senior NCO of the rated Soldier for comment and input. Upon receiving comments and input from the senior rater the rater will provide a copy of gtthe support form to the rated NCO and notify them of changes

How does the rater determine the overall performance of the rated NCO for the rating period?

This performance is evaluated in terms of the rated NCOs performance compared against other NCOs of the same rank that the rater has or is rating

The senior rater should reserve the "Most Qualified" box to identify what type of NCO?

Only those NCOs identified as the upper tier for each ranks

Rater tendency reports are not maintained on what level of NCO?

SGT and below

What is the maximum percentage of "Most Qualified" ratings a senior rater can have for NCOs?

24% or less

What does the term stand-alone mean with regard to evaluation reports?

Each evaluation report will address a specific period of time and not be related to other reports

What date is used to determine if a performance issue or incident occured within the rated period?

The rating chain will use the date of the actual incident or performance issue to determine if the issue should be included in the rating period

When an NCO returns from a professional development school where they have achieved honor graduate, in which block of the evaluation report should this achievment be annotated?

This information cannot be entered on the evaluation report as it is already contained in the academic evaluation report and is considered non-rated time for the NCOER report

What achievement or comments from an academic evaluation report can be entered on an NCOER?

No comments from an academic evaluation report may be entered on a NCOER

When are hand-written comments acceptable for an NCOER evaluation report?

Handwritten comments are never acceptable on a NCOER evaluation report

What comment will be entered on an evaluation report if a Soldier recieves an Article 15 that is filed in the restricted portion of their Army Military Human Resources Record or filed locally at the unit?

No reference can be made concerning the Article 15 itself. However, this does not preclude mentioning the misconduct that led to the Article 15 being imposed

Can information from outside the rating period be used for a Relief for Cause report?

Yes, using reports outside the current rating period may be necessary to explain the reason for the relief

Can comments about a Soldier's marital status and/or spouse be mentioned in an evaluation report?

Yes, but only in rare occasions when necessary to properly evaluate the rated Soldier's perrformance and potential

If the following comment appropriate for an evaluation report:
"As a bachelor, 1SG Smith can quickly react to the unit's contingency missions?"

No. This comment would be prohibited because it is based soley on the Soldier's marital status

If the following comment appropriate for an evaluation report:
"Mrs. Smith's participation in the unit activities is limited by her civilian employment?"

No. This comment would be prohibited because it is based on a spuse's employment

Is the following comment appropriate on an evaluation report
"1SG Smith's belligerent confrontations with his wife were detrimental to the unit's mission and his status as a senior leader"

Yes. This comment is permitted as it focuses on the rated Soldier's behavior and how it impacted performance and/or his potential

If a Soldier voluntarily enters the ASAP program for a situation not detected by the chain of command, what comments will be placed on the rated Soldier's evaluation report?

NO comment will be placed on the Soldier's evaluation report. The Soldier will not be penalized by mentioning participation in the ASAP program if they voluntarily entered the program and the condition was not detected by the chain of command

What comments should the rating chain make concerning the rated Soldier's rehabilitation progress or outcome in the ASAP program?

The rating chain should note the status of the Soldier's progress or outcome in the current evaluation or in later evaluation reports

If a Soldier voluntarily enters mental health counseling and successfully completes treatment to remedy the original behavioral health issues, what type of comment might be entered on the evaluation report?

A comment will be made as a factor to the Solder's credit

When is the rated Soldier's participation in an official or unofficial investigation protected under the whistleblower protection act mentioned in an evaluation report?

The Soldier's participation in an investigation is protected under the whistleblower protection act and will never be mentioned on Army evaluation reports

How long does a Soldier have to submit an evaluation report?

Substantiated appeals must be submitted within three years of an evaluation report "thru" date

What is a complete record report?

A report intended to update the Soldier's' file with performance and potential information not previously documented since the last evaluation report

Define pooling as it relates to evaluation reports?

When a senior official rate individuals of a specific rank that would normally be rated by a subordinate leader for the purpose of creating a larger population, so the senior official has a bigger population to rate. Therefore, allowing the senior official the ability to give out ratings they would not normally be allowed to if their population was smaller

Who is the supplementary reviewer for an NCO in the rank of SFC through 1SG/MSG?

A uniformed Army Advisor, senior to the senior rater in the rank of SGM//CSM or an Army officer in the rank of CPT or above

If a rated Soldier has 60 days rated time and 30 days nonrated time can they recieve an evaluation report?

No. The Soldier must have 90 days of rated time. The 30 days of nonrated time cannot be counted towards the 90-day rating period

When is an Army Uniformed Advisor required to conduct a supplementary review?

When the rater and senior rater are not uniformed members

During a "Relief for Cause" report who conducts the review if there are no Army officers or Uniformed Army Advisors in the chain of command directing the relief?

The senior rater will request that the HQDA perform the review

If a rater dies, is declared missing, relieved, or becomes mentally or physically incapable of rendering an objective report and the minimum 90-day rating period has NOT been met what action is taken?

The time is counted as nonrated time

if a rater dies, is declared missing, relieved, or becomes mentally or physically incapable of rendering an objective and the minimum 90-day rating period HAS been met what action will be taken?

The senior rater will perform the rater's function but only if they feel qualified to rate teh Soldier and have served in the rating position for 60 or more calender days

What are the evaluation requirements for a retirement NCOER?

All Soldiers will recieve an NCOER within 12 months before the first day of the transitional leave. NCOERs for less than one calender year are optional

How does the rater determine the rating for potential on the evaluation report?

The NCOs ability will be compared to that of their contemporaries. This assesment of potential applies to all NCOs regardless of their opportunity to be selected for a higher positions or grade

What "Potential" block should a senior rater select if they believe the NCO has potential for promotion with further development?

They should place an X in the Qualified box

What NCO ranks are tracked on the senior rater profile?

SSG through CSM

When can a senior rater request a restart of their senior rater profile?

1. When three NCOERs have been processed against that grade at HQDA
2. The senior rater has obtained permission or authorization from their senior rater
3. The senior rater has a documented misfire in the grade for restart

What performance or incident will not be contained within an evaluation report?

Incidents or performances that occured prior to, after, or during periods of non-rated time will not be noted on the evaluation report

Give some examples regarding incidents that will not be noted on the evaluation report

1. A Relief for Cause report based on information pertaining to a previous reporting period
2. When the most recent APFT (ACFT) performance or profile data occured prior to the beginning of the evaluation report but within 12 months of the through date of the evaluation report
3. A substantiated EO, EEO, and SHARP complaint as a result of AR 15-6 investigation
4. When a Soldier assigned to a WTU designation on a valid rating chain and recieves an NCOER with a non-rated code of G

If an acronym is not found in the authorized abbreviation, brevity codes, and acronym database how is the acronym written in a NCOER evaluation report?

The abbreviation or acronym must be spelled out completely the first time it is used. the abbreviation is then entered in parantheses immediately after the spelled-out version. Afterwards, the abbreviation may be used in the evaluation report

Where can a list of authorized abbreviations, brevity codes, and acronyms can be found?

This information can be found on the Army publishing directorates website

A senior rater's profile for SSG "Most Qualified" is already at the maximum 24% mark. This means no additional SSGs can be rated as "Most Qualified". However, the senior rater has an exceptional NCO and makes the following comment on the evaluation report, "SGT Snuffy is one of the most qualified leaders I have served with; a 6+ leader, definite select for SFC in the secondary zone". Is this an acceptable comment for an evaluation report?

No, this comment is in violation of AR 623-3. No reference can be made to a box that the senior rater's profile cannot support. In addition, specific selection board type language like "6+ Soldier" is also prohibited

A Soldier is currently under investigation for an incident. The investigation is not complete. What comment if any will be entered on the evaluation report?

No reference to the investigation or incident will be mentioned on the evaluation report as it is considered unproven derogatory information until the investigation is complete.

What type of comment can be placed on an evaluation report if a Soldier complains to a member of United States Congress?

No comment will be placed on the Soldiers evaluation report beacuse it is considered protected communication

Name two types of protected communication?

1. Communication with the Inspector General
2. A member of Congress
3. A court-martial
4. A member of the chain of command designated to recieve protected communications

Is the following comment permitted on an evaluation report:
"1SG Smith exhibited outstanding leadership and selfless service during his wife's severe illness"

Yes, the comment would be permitted as it focuses on the rated Soldier's performance and not the action of the spouse

Name four of the eight behaviors that can be mentioned on an evaluation report, IAW 600-20, when substantiated by an investigation

1. Criminal acts
2. Conviction of DUI
3. Acts of sexual misconduct or physical or mental abuse
4. Inapproriate or unprofessional personal relationships
