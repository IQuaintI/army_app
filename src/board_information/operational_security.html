Operational Security (OPSEC) (AR 530-1)

Home

Define operational security

A process that identifies critical information of military plans and indicators that might reveal it, then develops measures to eliminate, reduce, or conceal these indicators

What is critical information?

Important information to the success of US missions which may be of use to an enemy of the United States. If an enemy where to obtain it and correctly analyze it, the mission could ne jepordized

What can occur from OPSEC violations?

1. Serious injury or death
2. Damage to weapons systems, equipments, and facilities
3. Loss of sensitive technologies
4. Mission failure

Who is the authority for implementing OPSEC?

The commander

What type of information does OPSEC protect?

Classified or unclassified

What is physical security?

Protective measures to deny unauthorized personnel access to specific areas, facilities, materials, or classified information

What is force protection?

Actions taken to prevent or mitigate hostile actions against all DoD personnel, resouces, facilities, and critial information

What is COMSEC?

Communication Security

Define signature as it applies to OPSEC

Make an indicator identifiable or causes it to stand out

Define association

Key to interpretation by comparing current information with past information to identify possible relationships
