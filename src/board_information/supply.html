What does CSDP stand for?

Command Supply Discipline Program

Define CSDP

A commander's program which leaders enforce to ensure resources are not subject to fraud, waste, and abuse

Who is responsible for enforcing the CSDP?

It is an individual, supervisory, and managerial responsibility

Can government property be sold, given as a gift, loaned, exchanged, used for private purposes or otherwise be disposed if?

Of

Define supply discipline

The compliance with established DA regulations to effectively administer supply economy. Supply discipline applies to all functions and levels of supply (from unit/contractor through national users) and to the effective use of supply funds

What is contained in Class I?

Food and water

What is contained in Class III?

Fuel, oil, and lubricants

What is contained in Class V?

Ammunition

Define property accountability

It is the use, care, custody, and safekeeping of organized clothing and individual equipment

What does OCIE stand for?

Organized clothing and individual equipment

Can Army property be used for private purposes?

No except as authorized by HQ department of the Army

Give some examples of organizational property

OCIE, CTA 50, special tools and equipment, and basic loads

Define the life cycle of property accountability?

Accounting will be continuous from time of acquisition until ultimate consumption or disposal of the property occurs

What are the classifications of Army property?

Non-expendable property
Expendable property
Durable property

What are the types of property?

Organizational and installation

Define real property

Consists of lands and permanent structures

Define personal property

Made up of equipment and other non-expendable supplies, collectively called non-consumables supplies, all consumable supplies, and relocatable buildings

Define non-expendable property

Personal property that is not consumed in use and that retains its original identity during the period of use (e.g. weapons and tools)

Define expendable property

Property consumed in use, or loses it's identity in use. Includes items not consumed in use with a unit cost of less than $500.00 (e.g. office supplies, oil, and fuel)

Define durable property

Personal property that is not consumed in use and that does not require property book accountability but requires control when issued to the user (e.g. computers and software)

What are the five types of responsibility for CSDP?

1. Command
2. Supervisory
3. Direct
4. Custodial
5. Personal

What does FLIPL stand for

Financial Liability Investigation of Property Loss

In the FLIPL process, what four elements must be proven to hold a Soldier financially liable?

1. Loss, damage, or destruction
2. Responsibility
3. Culpability
4. Proximate cause

What is a FLIPL?

It is a manner in which the Army accounts for the circumstances surrounding the loss, damage, or destruction

Unless you fall into a special category, what is the most you wil be required to pay under the FLIPL process?

One month's pay

Generally the FLIPL process must be completed in hw many days?

75 days, however these are flexible but should be abided by as much as possible

When does the time start with regards to the time limit to complete a FLIPL?

When the loss or damage is discovered

How long does a Soldier have a submit a rebuttal to a FLIPL?

Seven calender days

If a 15-6 investigation is being conducted in conjunction with a FLIPL what must happen before the FLIPL is given to the Soldier for review?

The 15-6 investigation must be completed before the Soldier is given notice and an opportunity to rebut

Define supply economy

The conservation of material by every individual dealing with the Army supplies to ensure that only the proper item in the necessary amount is used to accomplish a task. The term stewardship of resources is synonymous with supply economy

Define accountability as it pertains to supply operations

Accountability pertains to maintaining formally prescribed property records for a property or sales account. It is an obligation officially assigned to a specific person and may not be delegated.

What action must an individual take when they receive property on a hand-receipt or sub-hand receipt?

They must check all items and descriptions
Make a list of any discrepancies
Count all items
Verify serial numbers
Report discrepancies
Receipts are handed over to the assigned individual

When the hand-receipt holder is replaced, how long does the primary hand-receipt holder have to conduct a joint inventory?

30 days

How often are sensitive items inventoried?

Quarterly with no more than one quarter of the year between inventories
