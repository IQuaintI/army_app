What is the treatment for human or animal bites?

1. Cleanse wounds thoroughly with soap or detergent solution
2. Flush bite well with water
3. Cover bite with sterile dressing
4. Immobilize injured extremity
5. Transport casualty to a medical treatment facility
6. For human bites try to extract some of the attackers saliva from the wound and send it in a sealed identified container with casualty
7. For animal bites, kill the animal without endangering yourself or damaging the animal's head
8. Send animal head with casualty

What are the signs and symptoms for a fire red ant sting?

1. Intense, fiery, burning pain
2. Circular pattern of bites
3. Very painful area that may contain swelling
4. Cloudy fluid bubble within two to four hours
5. Bubble with a red ring around it at around eight to ten hours
6. Ulceration with scarring

How fast does anaphylactic shock set in?

Within seconds or minutes

What is the treatment for anaphylactic shock?

Administer the casualty with their auto injector of epinephrine

What are some signs and symptoms of anaphylactic shock?

Skin:
1. Flushed or ashen skin
2. Burning or itching sensations
3. Edema (swelling) especially around the eyes, lips, and tongue
4. Urticaria (hives) spreading over the body
Respiratory:
1. Tightness in the chest
2. Wheezing, difficulty of breathing
3. Sneezing and/or coughing
4. Spit (may be bloody)
Circulatory:
1. Weak, rapid pulse
2. Falling blood pressure
3. Dizziness, fainting, coma
4. High blood pressure

What are the signs and symptoms of a bee, wasp, hornet, or yellow jacket stings (severe reactions)?

1. Itching and burning
2. Swelling around lips and tongue
3. Hives
4. Tightness of chest
5. Narrowing of airway and wheezing
6. Shortness of breath
7. Abdominal cramps
8. Anxiety

What is the treatment for a wasp or bee sting?

1. If the stinger is present, remove it by scraping with a knife or fingernail
2. DO NOT squeeze venom sack on stinger as more venom may be released
3. Wash the area
4. Apply ice to the area
5. If allergic signs or symptoms appear, be prepared to administer an antidote and seek further medical attention

In the United States, wnat is the only deadly type of scorpion?

The Arizona black scorpion

What are the signs and symptoms of a deadly scorpion bite?

1. Sharp pain at the injection site
2. Pins and needles sensation
3. Severe muscle contractions
4. Drooling
5. Poor circulation
6. High blood pressure
7. Cardiac failure
8. Incontinence
9. Seizures

What are the signs and symptoms of a harmless scorpion bite?

1. Severity depends on amount of venom injected
2. Sharp pains at the injection site
3. Lasts for 24-72 hours

What actions should be avoided when treating a snake bite?

Do not scrub the area or apply a cold pack

With regards to a snake bite, should first aid or transport be delayed in order to capture the snake?

No, treatment and transport comes first

With regards to a snake bite, if you are able to kill the snake (while keeping the head intact), what should you do with the snake?

Place the snake in a sealed container and transport with the casualty

What is the treatment for a snake bite?

1. Get casualty away from snake
2. Remove all rings and bracelets from affected areas
3. Reasses the casualty
4. Keep them quiet
5. Apply a constricting band two to three inches above the bite area
6. Immobilize the affected area below heart level
7. Treat and monitor for shock
8. Kill snake (without damaging head) and send with casualty
9. Transport to medical facility and record actions on TCCC DD Form 1380

What should you NOT do in the event of a snake bite?

Do NOT cut the bite and suction or squeeze the bite site

What is the first aid procedures for a non-poisonous snake bite?

Wash the area around the bite using mild or strong soap solutions and sterile saline (if available) or clean water then transport for medical care

If a snake bite cannot be identified as non-poisonous, what actions should you take?

Always treat the bite as if poisonous and do not delay treatment

Describe how to identify a non-poisonous snake?

Four to six rows of teeth and no fangs

Describe how to identify a poisonous snake?

1. Two rows of teeth
2. Two fangs which create puncture wounds
3. Elliptical pupils or vertical slits, much like those of a cat
4. Pit between eye and mouth
5. Variety of different shaped blotches on backgrounds of pink, yellow, olive, tan, gray, or brown skin

What snake has no fangs but is extremely poisonous?

Coral snake

What are the identifying marks of the coral snakes?

Ringed with red, yellow, and black (Red on yellow will kill a fellow)

What are the signs and symptoms of a brown recluse spider bite?

1. Casualty rarely recalls being bitten
2. Hours later the bite becomes bljuish and painful
3. A red halo or bulls eye pattern
4. Within seven to ten days the bite area becomes ulcer

What are the signs and symptoms of a black widow spider bite?

1. A pinprick sensation that becomes a dull ache within 30 minutes
2. Painful cramps in the abdomen, shoulders, back, and neck that begin at around 10 - 40 minutes
3. Lasts for 12 - 48 hours and abdomen becomes rigid
4. Dizziness, nausea, vomiting, and respiratory distress

What is the treatment for a brown recluse or black widow spider bite?

1. Calm the casualty
2. Wash the area
3. Apply ice or freeze pack
4. Seek medical treatment

Other than Lyme disease, what can a tick bite cause?

1. Tick fever
2. Rocky Mountain spotted fever
3. Other bacterial infections

What are the signs and symptoms of a tick bite?

1. Itching and redness at the site
2. Headaches
3. Moderate to high fever lasting two or three weeks
4. Pains in joint and legs
5. Swollen lymph nodes in the bitten area
6. Paralysis and other nervous system problems

How is Lyme disease transmitted

Usually by the tiny deer tick but possibly through the larger dog tick

How long must a tick remain attached to the body to transmit infection?

Four to six hours
