Retention (AR 601-280, DA PAM 601-280, TC 7-21.13)

Home

Where can you find information on bars to continued service?

AR 601-280 chapter 8

What is the correct terminology for a bar?

Bar to continued service

Is a bar to re-enlistment an administrative or punitive action?

Punitive

Does imposing a bar to continued service prevent administrative separation at a later date?

No, the Soldier can still be administrated separated at a later date

When a bar to continued service is imposed upon a Soldier, what information must be provided?

They will be advised exactly what is expected of them to overcome the bar and will be given explicit timetables to overcome the reason for the bar

When can a bar be initiated on a Soldier found fit for duty by a physical evaluation board, but is found unsuitable for deployment or worldwide assignment?

A bar will note be initiated on these Soldiers. Only the SECDEF can deny re-enlistment to these Soldiers

What action will be taken against any Soldier who fails to qualify for mandatory appearance before a promotion board upon attaining primary zone time in service/time in grade eligibility because they did not complete mandatory Structured Self-Development Courses?

A bar to continued service will be initiated and forwarded to the DCS G-1 who will impose a HQDA bar

Upon completion of each three-month review period for Soldiers fully eligible for promotion in the primary zone, who failed to complete the required SSD/DLC course, what action must the commander take?

The soldier will be counseled using the DA FORM 4856 to inform the Soldier that the bar has been reviewed, and will remain in effect unless the Soldier completes the mandatory SSD/DLC courses

What action must a commander take, if after the second three-month review period, the bar is no recommended for removal?

The commander will initiate separation proceedings under AR 635-200

How should a Soldier be evaluated when a commander is considering imposing a bar?

Under the Whole Person Concept

What is the process for barring a Soldier if they refuse to re-enlist?

Soldiers cannot be barred if they refuse to re-enlist

If a Soldier received an Article 15 or was going to be separated and was later retained can they be bared from continued service, if so explain?

Yes, any documentation from the Article 15 or separation packet can be used to initiate a bar to continued service

What does ARP stand for?

Army Retention Program

The ARP is comparable to what other Army program?

The Quality Management Program (QMP)
