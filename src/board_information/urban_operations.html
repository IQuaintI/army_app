What does the term UO stand for?

Urban Operations

What does the term MOUT stand for?

Military Operations on Urbanized Terrain.

What is our mission when conducting MOUT operations?

Defeat destroy the enemy above all other considerations.

What is our mission when conducting UO operations?

Defeat the enemy, gain and retain the good will of the population.

What does MTT stand for?

Mobile Training Teams.

Define Risk Assessment.

Risk Assessment means making operations safe without compromising the level of realistic training.

What does a proper risk assessment during UO operations ensure?

UO training is not only tough and realistic, but also safe.

What are the methods used to train soldiers in urban operations?

Crawl, walk, run.

How was the training accomplished during crawl phase of UO operations?

The trainer talks to soldiers through the exercise step-by-step.

How was training accomplished during the walk phase of UO operations?

Soldiers slowly practice each task to standard. Trainers coach soldiers at this stage, stopping as often as needed to correct mistakes and provide feedback.

How was training accomplished during the run phase of UO operations?

Soldiers perform each task at full speed as if they are in combat.

What does MAPEX stand for?

Map Exercise.

What does FTX stand for?

What type of enemy are we likely to face in a MOUT situation?

Conventional Russian type.

What type of enemy are we likely to face in a UO situation?

Insurgents or unconventional.

When conducting UO operations, what consideration is given to the population?

They are considered critical to mission success.

What are the three training phases of UO operations?

Initial, intermediate, culminating.

What characterizes intermediate UO training?

Collective training in varied conditions such as limited visibility, entry techniques, etc.

What training is incorporated into the culmination phase of UO operations?

Can include multi-escalant combined arms and branch-specific STX training may be conducted.

Define the term training enhancers with regards to UO operations.

Life fire, blank immunizations, simulators, fires, smoke, and other pyrotechnics.

What does prerequisite training do with regards to a team involved in UO operations?

Builds teamwork and individual confidence at all levels.

When conducting MOUT operations, what consideration is given to the population?

Individual and collective tasks

Improves the performance of the leaders and soldiers in later iterations by allowing interaction and feedback from these individuals during the debrief.

What is the purpose of TEWT exercise?

It focuses on leader and staff tasks as training.

What is the purpose of a CPX exercise?

It allows leaders and battle staffs to perform their tasks in a unit CP.

What is the purpose of a STX exercise?

STXs are mission-oriented limited exercises used to train a unit to perform a single collective task or a series of related tasks and drills.

What is the purpose of an FTX exercise in UO operations?

It allows units to execute collaborative tasks under conditions that closely resemble actual urban terrain.
