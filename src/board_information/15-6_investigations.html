What regulation covers Procedures for Administrative Investigations and Boards of Officers(15-6 Investigations)?

AR 15-6

What does the acronym IO mean?

Investigating Officer

What action must be taken against any Soldier under an investigation?

They must flagged under AR 600-8-2

If an adverse finding is made against a Soldier at the conclusion of the 15-6 investigation, what option is available to the Soldier?

The Soldier has the right to appeal the finding upon discovery of new evidence, mistake of law, mistake of fact, or administrative error

How long does the Soldier have to request reconsideration for an adverse 15-6 finding?

Up to one year from the approval authority's approval of the investigation

How are "Off the Record" comments handled in a 15-6 investigation?

There are no "Off the Record" conversations. These comments cannot be considered for substance but only as help in finding additional evidence

When must an individual be informed of their rights during a 15-6 investigation?

When they are suspected of criminal misconduct and prior questioning

How are investigating officers appointed?

In writing

Who should not appoint an inquiry or investigation?

An individual who is reasonably likely to become a witness to an inquiry, investigation, or board may not appoint an inquiry, investigation, or board

If an individual has an actual or perceived bias in the case or perceived conflict of interest in the outcome of an investigation, they should not take what type of action?

An individual who has an actual or perceived bias for or against a potential subject of the investigation, or an actual or perceived conflict of interest in the outcome of the investigation should not appoint an inquiry or investigation

Who will be appointed as an investigation officer?

Those persons who, in the opinion of the appointing authority, are best qualified for the duty by reason of their education, training, experience, length of service, demonstrated sound judgement and temperment

What enlisted grades if any can be assigned as an investigating officer?

E-7 or above

What are the qualifications the investigating officer must exhibit to ensure the investigation is completed to standard?

The Io must be impartial, unbiased, objective, and have the ability to complete the investigation in a timely manner

Who can be appointed as an investigating officer?

Only commissioned officers, warrant officers, and Department of the Army civilian employees permanently assigned to a position graded as GS-11 or above may be appointed as IO. When these are not available Non-commissioned officers in the grade of E-7 or above may be appointed as IOs

After the IO has completed their investigation what action must be taken to finalize the report?

It must go through a legal review by the servicing legal officer or advisor

Can the leader (approval authority), who recieves the report modify or reject the IO's findings, if so explain?

Yes. The approval authority may approve, disapprove, modify, or add to the findings and recommendations, consistent with the evidence included in the report of the proceedings

Can the approval authority implement action not recommended or contained in the final investigation report, if so explain?

Yes. The approval authority may take action different than that recommended with regard to a respondent or other individual

What criteria must the investigating officer's recommendeds meet?

The recomendations must be clearly written and should be feasible, acceptable, and suitable

Where can the investigating officer's recomendations meet?

AR-15-6 Appendix C
