Define weapon safety status.

A standard code using the colors green, amber, red, or black to represent the level of readiness for a given weapon.

What does the weapon safety status "green" mean on the M2 machine gun?

Trigger on safe, bolt in single shot position, remove weapon's belts, ensure the chamber and T-slot are empty and the bolt is forward.

What does the weapon safety status "amber" mean on the M2 machine gun?

Trigger on safe, bolt latch release in the single shot or automatic position. Load a belt of ammo into the feed until the belt holding pawl holds the first cartridge, the chamber, and the T-slot are empty and the bolt is forward.

What does the weapon safety status "red" mean on the M2 machine gun?

Trigger on safe, bolt latch release in the single shot or automatic position. Load a belt of ammunition into the feed-way until the first cartridge is held by the belt holding pawls then the gun is charged twice moving a round into the chamber, the bolt is forward and locked.

What does the weapon safety status "black" mean on the M2 machine gun?

A belt of ammo is located into the feed-way until the first cartridge is held by the belt holding pawls of the weapon, then the gun is charged twice, moving a round into the chamber. Moving a round into the chamber, bolt is forward and locked. Trigger is set to fire, bolt latch releases in the single shot or automatic position, soldier's thumbs are positioned to fire the weapon.
